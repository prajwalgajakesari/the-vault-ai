University of Hawaii researchers developed a physics-informed machine learning algorithm that constrains AI predictions to comply with fundamental laws of nature and physics. The approach improves scientific accuracy and reduces physically impossible predictions in complex systems modeling. This breakthrough is particularly valuable for climate science, materials research, and engineering applications. Physics-informed AI bridges the gap between deep learning flexibility and scientific rigor.
