# Utah First State to Allow AI to Renew Prescriptions

Utah has become the nation's first state to authorize AI to autonomously renew prescription medications for chronic conditions. The partnership between the Utah Department of Commerce and Doctronic launched in January 2026 within the state's regulatory sandbox framework.

"This partnership enables patients, pharmacists, and physicians to work together more efficiently," said Matt Pavelle, Doctronic Co-CEO. The pilot is limited to 190 commonly prescribed medications, excluding pain management drugs, ADHD medications, and injectables. A $4 charge per renewal funds the service.

Human physicians are reviewing the first 250 prescriptions within each medication class. Once validated, subsequent renewals are handled autonomously. Doctronic reported its AI matched physician treatment plans in 99.2% of 500 urgent care cases reviewed.

"Balance between fostering innovation and ensuring safety," Margaret Woolley Busse, Executive Director of the Utah Department of Commerce, stated as the guiding principle. The American Medical Association has warned that without physician input, AI prescription systems pose serious risks.

The pilot tracks five core metrics: refill timeliness, patient access, safety outcomes, workflow efficiency, and cost impacts. Arizona and Texas have created similar AI regulatory sandboxes. Prescription renewals account for roughly 80% of all medication activity nationally, and noncompliance costs the healthcare system over $100 billion annually.
