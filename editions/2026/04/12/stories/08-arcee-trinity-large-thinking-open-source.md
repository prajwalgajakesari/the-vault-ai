# Arcee Ships Trinity-Large-Thinking, 399B Open Model

Arcee's newly released Trinity-Large-Thinking model breaks new ground as a frontier-class reasoning model under the Apache 2.0 license. The 399-billion parameter text-only reasoning model, released on April 3, 2026, can be downloaded, customized, and self-hosted without proprietary restrictions.

Trinity-Large-Thinking operates on a sparse Mixture-of-Experts architecture with approximately 13 billion active parameters per token, running 2-3 times faster than dense models of comparable capability. Arcee invested nearly half its total funding, $20 million, into a single 33-day training run using 2,048 NVIDIA B300 Blackwell GPUs, training on 17 trillion tokens.

On PinchBench, Arcee's proprietary reasoning benchmark, Trinity-Large-Thinking reaches a score of 91.9, second only to Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.6 at 93.3. The cost comparison is striking: $0.90 per million output tokens versus Claude Opus 4.6 at $25, a 96-percent cost reduction.

"Frontier open reasoning model under Apache 2.0," Arcee stated. The predecessor model, Trinity-Large-Preview, established itself as the number-one most-used open model in the United States on OpenRouter, serving over 80.6 billion tokens on peak days.

The model excels at multi-turn tool calling, context coherence, instruction following, and stable behavior across long-running agent loops. For enterprises hesitant about proprietary models due to cost, vendor lock-in, or data privacy, Trinity-Large-Thinking offers a credible alternative. The 96-percent cost advantage forces a reckoning across the industry.
