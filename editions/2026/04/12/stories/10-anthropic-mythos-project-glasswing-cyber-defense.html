Anthropic Deploys Mythos Preview via Project Glasswing

Anthropic withheld most powerful AI model for cyber defense via Glasswing.

Anthropic unveiled Project Glasswing, a tightly controlled initiative deploying Claude Mythos Preview exclusively to a coalition of major technology and finance firms to hunt critical software vulnerabilities before attackers do.

"We do not plan to make Claude Mythos Preview generally available due to its cybersecurity capabilities," Anthropic stated. The company is committing up to $100 million in usage credits paired with $4 million in donations to open-source security organizations.

The initiative launches with twelve partners: Amazon Web Services, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft, Nvidia, and Palo Alto Networks, plus over 40 additional organizations. In just weeks, Anthropic has identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major operating system and web browser, including a 27-year-old bug in OpenBSD.

"Autonomy and long-ranged characteristics of this model" represent a paradigm shift, according to Logan Graham, Anthropic's Offensive Cyber Research Lead. Mythos Preview can autonomously chain together multiple vulnerabilities into sophisticated end-to-end exploits. Security experts said developing such exploits manually would take weeks; Mythos Preview wrote them in hours.

Project Glasswing assumes that some tools are too dangerous to democratize, even temporarily. Anthropic's willingness to spend $100 million and forego immediate commercial revenue suggests genuine belief that defensive benefits outweigh business costs.
