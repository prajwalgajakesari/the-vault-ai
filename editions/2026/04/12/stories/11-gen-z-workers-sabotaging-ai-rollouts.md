# Survey: 44% of Gen Z Workers Sabotaging Employer AI

Nearly half of all Gen Z workers are deliberately undermining their company's AI initiatives. A report from Writer and Workplace Intelligence surveyed 2,400 knowledge workers across the U.S., U.K., and Europe. While 29% of all employees admit to sabotaging their company's AI strategy, that number rockets to 44% among Gen Z workers.

The sabotage entails entering proprietary information into public AI tools, using unapproved tools, and intentionally degrading output. Of those who admitted to sabotaging, 30% cited fear that AI would take their job, dubbed "FOBO" (fear of becoming obsolete).

The irony is cutting: 60% of executives told researchers they're considering cutting employees who refuse to adopt AI. A 52-point chasm exists between worker and executive confidence: only 9% of workers trust AI for complex decisions, compared to 61% of executives.

Despite rising resistance, Gen Z's actual AI usage climbed from 47% daily or weekly in 2025 to 51% in 2026. The share who say AI makes them excited plummeted from 36% to 22%, while measured anger rose 9 points to 31%.

Seventy-seven percent of executives warn that employees who refuse to become AI-proficient won't be considered for promotions. Until leadership addresses the legitimate anxieties driving resistance and offers concrete assurances about job preservation, the insurgency will likely deepen.
