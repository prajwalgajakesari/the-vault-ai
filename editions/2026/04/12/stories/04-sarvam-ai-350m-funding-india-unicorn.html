Sarvam AI Nears \$350 Million Raise at \$1.5 Billion Valuation

India's largest AI startup funding round with backing from major investors.

India's AI landscape is experiencing a seismic shift as Sarvam AI approaches completion of what could become the largest funding round for an Indian AI startup. The New Delhi-based startup is nearing a $300 million to $350 million raise at a valuation between $1.5 billion and $1.55 billion.

Founded in 2023 by Vivek Raghavan and Pratyush Kumar, Sarvam AI builds voice-first artificial intelligence systems optimized for India's linguistic and commercial landscape. Bessemer Venture Partners is leading the investment round, with strategic participation from Nvidia, Amazon, and Prosperity7 Ventures.

"Bring AI to a billion Indians," Pratyush Kumar, co-founder, has stated as the company's mission. Sarvam AI's ascent accelerated following the company's February showcase at the India AI Impact Summit, where it unveiled two large language models with 30 billion and 105 billion parameters, both trained entirely within India.

The company's focus on supporting 22 Indian languages addresses a fundamental market opportunity overlooked by global AI leaders. For a country with over 1.4 billion people speaking hundreds of languages, this represented both a gap and an opportunity.

In December 2023, Sarvam closed a combined seed and Series A round totaling approximately $41 million. This Series B round represents institutional conviction at scale. Nvidia's participation is noteworthy as the company controls the GPU supply chain underpinning AI model training and inference.

India's government has increasingly emphasized AI as a strategic priority. Sarvam AI's success validates that approach, showing Indian capital, talent, and training methodologies can produce world-competitive AI systems. If this funding round closes as expected, Sarvam AI will join an exclusive club of Indian AI startups at unicorn valuation.
