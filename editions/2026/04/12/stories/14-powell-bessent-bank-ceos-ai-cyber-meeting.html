Powell and Bessent Summon Bank CEOs for AI Cyber Briefing

Fed and Treasury convened bank CEOs for emergency meeting on AI cyber risks.

Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent called an emergency meeting with the nation's most powerful bank leaders to warn them about Anthropic's new Mythos model and its unprecedented cybersecurity threat to the financial system.

The gathering brought together the CEOs of Bank of America, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and Wells Fargo to Treasury headquarters in Washington, D.C. "Could become systemic threat if confidence shatters," said Jaret Seiberg, a TD Securities analyst.

The catalyst: Anthropic's Mythos model can surpass all but the most skilled humans at finding and exploiting software vulnerabilities, having found thousands of high-severity vulnerabilities in every major operating system and web browser.

Jamie Dimon, JPMorgan Chase's CEO, was notably absent due to previously scheduled travel. Nearly every other major banking CEO dropped their Tuesday morning obligations to attend the classified briefing.

Powell emphasized that banks must immediately assess their vulnerability to AI-enhanced exploits. Bank executives left with explicit directives: fortify network defenses, accelerate patching schedules, increase monitoring, and establish protocols for AI-enhanced cyber incidents.

The emergency meeting reflects a broader shift in how regulators perceive AI risk. Powell made clear that federal banking regulators will increase oversight of banks' AI-related cybersecurity postures, with potential enforcement implications.
