Google AI Mode Expands Agentic Booking to 8 Countries

Google transforms Search from discovery tool into execution platform.

Google is rolling out agentic restaurant booking capabilities to eight new markets globally while signaling broader ambitions for transaction-enabled search. AI Mode will now allow users in Australia, Canada, Hong Kong, India, New Zealand, Singapore, South Africa, and the United Kingdom to book restaurant tables directly through natural language requests.

"We're starting to roll out today with finding restaurant reservations, and expanding soon to local service appointments and event tickets," Google stated. The system operates through partnerships with OpenTable, Resy, Tock, Ticketmaster, StubHub, SeatGeek, and Booksy.

Behind the scenes, the technology relies on Project Mariner, Google's web-browsing AI, the Knowledge Graph, and Maps data. Google is simultaneously expanding AI Mode access to 180+ countries and territories. The competitive stakes are substantial: if Google captures booking requests upstream within search, it shifts the entire economics of the hospitality ecosystem.

For restaurant operators and booking platforms, the expansion presents both opportunity and threat. Platforms like OpenTable gain access to Google's massive search traffic but cede control of the user experience. Google's expansion to local service appointments and event tickets suggests a more aggressive timeline than previous rollouts.

“Search evolves from discovery into execution platform.”
