# Accenture Invests in Replit to Scale AI-Driven Software Development

Accenture announced it has invested in Replit, an AI-powered cloud-based coding platform, through Accenture Ventures, signaling a deepening commitment to democratizing AI-driven development across its Fortune 500 client base.

"Every enterprise wants to move faster, from idea to working application," Ram Ramalingam, an Accenture executive, said in a statement. Replit boasts over 50 million users worldwide and maintains a presence at 85% of Fortune 500 companies. Enterprise teams at Atlassian, Adobe, Databricks, and Zillow already rely on Replit to build applications.

"Our mission has always been to make software creation accessible to anyone," said Ghazi Masood, Chief Revenue Officer at Replit. The platform combines a browser-based coding environment with AI-powered development assistance, collaboration tools, and cloud hosting in a single integrated workspace.

The timing reflects broader industry shifts. Enterprises increasingly recognize that traditional software development workflows are becoming impediments to rapid innovation. AI-assisted development promises to collapse timelines, translating natural language into functional code.

As part of the partnership, Accenture and Replit will collaborate to identify practical use cases and develop new workflows scaled across Accenture's global client base. Accenture's backing gives Replit significant credibility within the enterprise segment, where purchasing decisions are made by risk-averse executives.

The broader context matters. Vendors are competing intensely to become the default platform for AI-assisted coding. If the partnership succeeds in helping Fortune 500 companies safely adopt AI-assisted development at scale, it could accelerate the broader industry transition from human-centric coding to human-AI collaboration.
