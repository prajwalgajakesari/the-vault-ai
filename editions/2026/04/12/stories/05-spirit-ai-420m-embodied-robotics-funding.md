# Spirit AI Raises $420 Million for Embodied Robotics

Hangzhou-based robotics startup Spirit AI has secured $420 million in funding across two back-to-back rounds completed within 30 days. The financing features backing from Yunfeng Capital and Shunwei Capital, funds affiliated with Jack Ma and Lei Jun, alongside Sequoia China, HongShan Capital, and regional investment vehicles.

Founded in 2024 by Han Fengtao and team members from UC Berkeley, Tsinghua University, and Peking University, Spirit AI has moved from inception to commanding a $1.4 billion valuation. "Real competitors are large companies," Han Fengtao has said of the competitive landscape.

The company's technology focuses on developing a "Universal Brain" for robotics, general-purpose models capable of perceiving and acting within the real world. Spirit AI has accumulated over 200,000 hours of robot interaction data and has committed to exceeding 1 million hours by end of 2026. Its Spirit v1.5 model topped the RoboChallenge global leaderboard in January 2026.

Spirit's robotic systems already operate on CATL production lines, the world's largest manufacturer of battery packs, completing over 1,000 battery insertion tasks with success rates exceeding 99%. Barista robots powered by Spirit's technology work in JD MALL stores.

The $420 million war chest gives Spirit AI resources to scale data collection and accelerate robotic deployments across new industries. The embodied AI sector faces genuine technical challenges, but with backing from China's top venture capital firms and robots already performing real work, Spirit AI has positioned itself as a serious contender.
