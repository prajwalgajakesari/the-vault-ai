# India Builds Sovereign AI Infrastructure With 12 Labs

India is racing to establish control over its AI infrastructure, backing 12 sovereign AI laboratories with billions in government funding. The IndiaAI Mission, with a total outlay of approximately $1.2 billion, is deploying over 10,000 GPUs through strategic partnerships.

"Frontier AI models powerful tool for 1.4 billion residents," NVIDIA noted. Yotta Data is deploying over 20,000 NVIDIA Blackwell Ultra GPUs through its Shakti Cloud platform, while L&T is constructing gigawatt-scale facilities in Chennai and Mumbai.

At the model layer, PARAM-2, a 17-billion-parameter multilingual model backed by the Department of Science and Technology, marked a major milestone. Sarvam.ai open-sourced its Sarvam-3 series in sizes of 3B, 30B, and 100B parameters trained for 22 Indic languages.

The necessity for sovereign infrastructure reflects three imperatives: data protection for sensitive information, economic leverage over foreign tech giants, and cultural and linguistic relevance for Indian languages.

Three AI Centres of Excellence in Healthcare, Agriculture, and Sustainable Cities provide sector-specific research focus. Budget 2025 announced a fourth Centre of Excellence for AI in education. Five National Centres of Excellence for Skilling will equip youth with industry-relevant expertise.

As the 12 sovereign AI labs come online and GPU deployments reach commercial scale, India is declaring that strategic AI infrastructure belongs in the hands of nation-states, not global cloud monopolies.
