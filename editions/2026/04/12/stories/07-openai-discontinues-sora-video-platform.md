# OpenAI Discontinues Sora, Ending AI Video Push

OpenAI announced on March 24, 2026, that it would discontinue Sora, its generative AI video creation platform. Just months after launching the service in late 2024, OpenAI is pulling the plug, a stunning reversal signaling the company's shift away from consumer-facing creative tools toward the more profitable enterprise AI market.

The shutdown will occur in two stages: the Sora web and mobile app experiences close on April 26, 2026, while the API will remain available until September 24, 2026. Users have until the April deadline to download their content.

Sora's user base peaked at approximately one million users before collapsing to fewer than 500,000 active accounts. More damaging was the staggering operational cost: roughly $1 million per day. "As compute demand grows, the Sora research team continues to focus on world simulation research to advance robotics," OpenAI stated.

The Disney partnership collapse underscores this shift. Disney had committed $1 billion to a partnership with OpenAI, learning about the shutdown less than an hour before the public announcement. Disney subsequently released a statement saying it "respects OpenAI's decision to exit the video generation business."

For the broader AI video generation landscape, Sora's exit does not mean the technology will disappear. Runway, Pika Labs, and other startups continue advancing AI video tools. But OpenAI's withdrawal suggests video generation may not represent the blockbuster application category some anticipated. The company's pivot signals confidence in language models, coding assistants, and reasoning engines as the engines of future growth.
