# AcuityMD Raises $80 Million Series C to Scale AI-Powered MedTech Sales Platform

Medical device companies have long struggled with a fragmented data landscape — scattered claims databases, siloed FDA filings, disconnected CRM systems — all while trying to get life-saving technology into the hands of surgeons faster. AcuityMD just raised $80 million to make that problem a relic of the past.

The Boston-based startup announced its Series C on Monday, valuing the company at $955 million and bringing its total funding to over $160 million. StepStone Group led the round, with existing backers Benchmark, Redpoint Ventures, ICONIQ, and Atreides Management all participating. The near-unicorn valuation marks a dramatic ascent for a company founded in 2019 that has quietly become the commercial intelligence backbone for much of the medical technology industry.

The Platform Powering MedTech Sales

AcuityMD has built what it calls a proprietary MedTech ontology — a continuously enriched knowledge graph that maps physicians, facilities, hospital networks, procedures, and reimbursement dynamics into a single, queryable system. The platform aggregates healthcare data from claims databases, FDA filings, and government records, then layers in customer-specific information like contracting data, product indications, and CRM activity to give sales teams unified market visibility.

The numbers tell the story of adoption: more than 400 medtech companies now use AcuityMD, including 16 of the top 20 global medical device manufacturers. Collectively, those customers have identified over $34 billion in pipeline opportunities through the platform — a figure that underscores just how much commercial value was previously hidden in data silos.

“AI will transform MedTech, but only with the right context, deeply embedded in the workflows where decisions are made,” said Mike Monovoukas, CEO and co-founder of AcuityMD.

AcuityAI: Agentic Intelligence for the Field

The centerpiece of AcuityMD’s forward strategy is AcuityAI, an agentic AI product now in open beta. Rather than serving up static dashboards, AcuityAI lets sales reps and commercial leaders query complex business questions in natural language and receive actionable, context-aware answers — think territory prioritization, market trend analysis, and opportunity identification, all surfaced in real time.

The shift from passive analytics to agentic AI represents a broader trend in enterprise software, where users increasingly expect tools that do not just present data but act on it. For medtech sales reps who spend their days navigating hospital systems and surgeon preferences, the difference between searching for an insight and having it delivered proactively can translate directly into revenue.

Mark Edwards, Senior Director of Sales Operations at Kuros Biosciences, a current AcuityMD customer, spoke to that shift: “AcuityAI has given our commercial team sharper answers, faster. Insights that used to take hours are now surfaced right when our reps need them, leading to richer conversations.”

Where the Money Goes

AcuityMD plans to deploy the new capital across three priorities. First, the company will accelerate its agentic AI capabilities, building specialized AI agents tailored to distinct commercial personas — sales reps, marketing teams, and commercial leadership each have different decision-making workflows that demand purpose-built intelligence. Second, AcuityMD will deepen its MedTech ontology, expanding the data model that gives the platform its structural advantage. Third, and perhaps most ambitiously, the company intends to expand beyond commercial execution into the broader medical technology product lifecycle — a move that could position it as the operating system for how medtech companies bring products to market.

“AcuityMD is scaling rapidly while continuing to invest thoughtfully in its people and platform,” said Hunter Somerville, Partner at StepStone Group.

The Bigger Picture

The raise arrives at a moment when vertical AI platforms — software deeply tailored to a specific industry — are attracting outsized investor attention. Unlike horizontal AI tools competing in crowded markets, companies like AcuityMD have found defensible lanes inside large, regulated industries where domain expertise is the moat.

The medical device market itself is massive, projected to exceed $600 billion globally by 2028, and the commercial infrastructure supporting it has been chronically underdigitized. Hospital adoption cycles remain notoriously slow, procurement processes are byzantine, and sales teams often operate with incomplete information about which physicians are performing which procedures at which facilities.

AcuityMD’s bet is that by owning the data layer — the ontology that connects all those dots — it becomes indispensable to the commercial workflow. At $955 million, the market is signaling that bet is paying off. The question now is whether AcuityMD can cross the unicorn threshold and deliver on the broader platform vision before the window of vertical AI enthusiasm narrows.

For an industry where getting the right device to the right surgeon at the right time can change patient outcomes, the stakes extend well beyond the balance sheet.
