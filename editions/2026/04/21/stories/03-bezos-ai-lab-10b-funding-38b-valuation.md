# Jeff Bezos Nears $10 Billion Funding Round for AI Lab at $38 Billion Valuation

**Barely five months after launching with $6.2 billion, Project Prometheus is poised to become one of the most richly valued AI startups on the planet -- and Wall Street heavyweights are lining up to write the checks.**

Jeff Bezos is on the verge of closing a $10 billion funding round for Project Prometheus, his secretive artificial intelligence laboratory focused on building models that understand the physical world. The deal would value the San Francisco-based startup at $38 billion, according to a report first published by the Financial Times and confirmed by Bloomberg on April 21.

JPMorgan Chase and BlackRock are among the institutional investors backing the round, marking a significant vote of confidence from two of the largest names in global finance. Once finalized, the raise will bring Project Prometheus's total funding to more than $16 billion -- a staggering sum for a company that did not exist before November 2025.

## From Moonshots to Main Street

Project Prometheus was co-founded by Bezos and Vikram “Vik” Bajaj, a physical chemist with a PhD from MIT who previously led ambitious projects at Alphabet’s Google X division, including early-stage work on Wing delivery drones and Waymo self-driving vehicles. Bajaj also co-founded Xaira Therapeutics, an AI-driven drug discovery company, before partnering with Bezos on what the pair describe as their most consequential bet yet.

The company’s mission statement -- “AI for the physical economy” -- signals an ambition that extends well beyond chatbots and text generators. Where most leading AI labs have focused on large language models trained on internet text and images, Prometheus is developing systems trained on real-world experimental data, robotics interactions, and engineering workflows. The goal is AI that comprehends material behavior, engineering tolerances, manufacturing processes, and the laws of physics themselves.

“We are building intelligence that doesn’t just predict the next word -- it predicts the next state of a physical system,” Bajaj said during a rare public appearance at a Stanford engineering symposium earlier this year. “The digital economy has been transformed by AI. The physical economy is next.”

The startup has rapidly scaled to over 120 employees, many recruited from the upper ranks of OpenAI, xAI, Meta, and Google DeepMind. Its target verticals span manufacturing, aerospace, robotics, drug discovery, and logistics automation -- sectors where AI adoption has historically lagged behind consumer technology.

## Wall Street’s $38 Billion Wager

The participation of JPMorgan and BlackRock underscores a broader shift in how institutional capital views the AI sector. Where venture capital firms once dominated early-stage AI funding, the sheer scale of capital required to train frontier models has drawn in banks, asset managers, and sovereign wealth funds.

“A $38 billion price tag five months out of the gate is a number the AI industry has written maybe a handful of times,” noted a senior analyst tracking AI capital expenditure trends. “What makes this different is the industrial thesis. Bezos isn’t chasing the next chatbot -- he’s betting that whoever owns AI for the physical world owns the next century of manufacturing.”

The valuation places Project Prometheus in rarefied territory alongside companies like OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI, though its focus on physical rather than digital AI represents a fundamentally different strategic bet. Manufacturing remains one of the least modernized sectors in terms of AI adoption, with production lines still relying heavily on fixed automation and manual tuning.

## Analysis: A Calculated Gamble on Industrial Transformation

Bezos’s track record lends credibility to an otherwise audacious undertaking. His investments in Blue Origin, Amazon’s robotics-heavy fulfillment centers, and the company’s custom chip program all point to a longstanding conviction that intelligence embedded in physical systems is the next great technological frontier.

But the risks are real. Project Prometheus is pursuing what analysts describe as a “manufacturing transformation vehicle” -- a structure designed to deploy capital, capability, and ownership simultaneously across physical assets including jet engine manufacturers and semiconductor fabricators. The competitive landscape is intensifying: NVIDIA’s Omniverse platform, Google DeepMind’s robotics division, and a wave of well-funded startups are all racing toward physical AI.

The path to monetization also remains uncertain. Training AI on proprietary industrial data is orders of magnitude more complex and expensive than scraping the open web. Specialized data on material behavior, engineering tolerances, and real-world physics is closely guarded by incumbents and difficult to collect at scale.

Still, the appetite from investors suggests the market sees something worth the premium. The convergence of advanced robotics, generative AI, and industrial automation represents a potential multi-trillion-dollar opportunity -- and Bezos, with his track record of patient, capital-intensive bets that eventually reshape entire industries, may be uniquely positioned to capture it.

## What Comes Next

The funding round is expected to close in the coming weeks, though terms have not been finalized. If completed at the reported valuation, Project Prometheus will rank among the five most valuable private AI companies in the world before shipping a single commercial product.

For the broader AI industry, the deal represents a turning point: the moment physical AI stopped being a research curiosity and became a category worth tens of billions in committed capital. Whether Bezos and Bajaj can deliver on that promise will be one of the defining questions of the AI era’s next chapter.
