# OpenAI Turns On Cost-Per-Click Ads in ChatGPT, Setting Bids at $3 to $5

OpenAI has quietly flipped a switch that could reshape the economics of AI-powered advertising. Just ten weeks after launching its first impression-based ads inside ChatGPT, the company is now offering cost-per-click (CPC) campaigns with bids set between $3 and $5 per click — a move that brings the world’s most popular AI chatbot squarely into competition with Google and Meta for performance marketing dollars.

The shift, first reported by Digiday on April 21, marks a pivotal expansion of OpenAI’s nascent advertising business. Until now, advertisers in the ChatGPT pilot program could only purchase ads on a cost-per-thousand-impressions (CPM) basis — a model that launched on February 9 at a premium $60 CPM. That price has since tumbled to roughly $25 in some cases, and OpenAI has slashed its minimum spend commitment from $250,000 down to $50,000, signaling an aggressive push to widen its advertiser base.

From Impressions to Clicks: Why It Matters

The introduction of CPC pricing is more than a billing tweak. It represents OpenAI’s clearest bid yet to attract the performance marketers who dominate online ad spending. Under the new model, advertisers can set maximum CPC bids, choose to optimize campaigns for either views or clicks, and provide “context hints” to target specific query types — all through an ads manager interface that is rapidly evolving toward self-serve capabilities.

“As OpenAI looks to increase spending, this is going to help advertisers directly compare their results on OpenAI to other major advertising platforms and manage their spend,” said Nicole Greene, VP Analyst at Gartner. The comment underscores a fundamental truth: until advertisers can benchmark ChatGPT ads against Google Search or Meta’s platforms on a like-for-like basis, large-scale budget allocation remains a tough sell.

The $3 to $5 CPC range positions ChatGPT squarely alongside — or slightly above — Google Search pricing for many categories, though Meta’s cost-per-clicks reportedly run three to five times cheaper than Google Search, according to ad technology firm Adthena.

A $100 Million Run Rate and Growing

OpenAI’s advertising ambitions are backed by real momentum. The company disclosed on March 26 that its ads pilot had crossed $100 million in annualized revenue — a milestone reached barely seven weeks after launch. Looking ahead, PYMNTS reported that OpenAI is forecasting $2.4 billion in ad revenue for 2026 and a staggering $11 billion for 2027.

Those projections help explain the urgency behind the CPC rollout. Ads currently appear in ChatGPT search results and conversational responses, giving brands placement inside the conversational flow that hundreds of millions of users engage with daily.

“LLMs are now starting to bridge that gap, as intent begins to build through the back-and-forth of prompt-driven conversations,” said Ashley Fletcher, CMO at Adthena, pointing to how conversational AI captures user intent in ways that traditional search boxes do not.

The Measurement Gap

Yet for all the enthusiasm, a critical vulnerability remains: measurement. OpenAI is only now recruiting its first advertising marketing science leader — a role that will encompass attribution models, incrementality testing, media mix modeling, and geo experimentation. By comparison, Uber hired its first measurement head in 2025, three years after launching ads, and Netflix began building measurement teams in 2023, one year after its ad-supported tier debuted.

“Google and Meta own industry measurement, and that’s the real moat, not the UI. Post-click is easy. Post-view at $60 CPM is hard,” said Robert Webster, founder of advertising consultancy TAU, highlighting the infrastructure gap OpenAI must close to justify premium pricing.

Early ad performance reporting has been limited, making ROI justification difficult for marketers accustomed to the granular attribution engines at Google and Meta. OpenAI has partnered with Criteo to bolster its ad tech stack, but independent verification tools and pixel-based attribution remain conspicuously absent.

What Comes Next

Claire Holubowskyj, Senior Research Analyst at Enders Analysis, framed the CPC launch as part of a broader strategic imperative: “OpenAI’s experimentation with CPC is driven largely by its need to maintain demand growth and build trust with advertisers, though declining CPMs and an expanding pilot are also factors.”

OpenAI has signaled that future campaign types may focus on deeper user actions — app downloads, purchases, and other conversions — though no timeline has been set. The company is also preparing for international expansion and a move toward a full auction-based pricing model.

The CPC launch arrives ahead of OpenAI’s anticipated initial public offering, adding a proven revenue diversification story to its pitch to investors. With ChatGPT now offering both impression and click-based pricing, the question is no longer whether AI chatbots will carry advertising — it is how fast the dollars will flow.
