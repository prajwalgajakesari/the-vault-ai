# SoundHound AI Acquires LivePerson for $250 Million, Creating Conversational AI Powerhouse

SoundHound AI is betting a quarter of a billion dollars that the future of customer service lies in erasing the line between talking and typing. The Santa Clara-based voice AI company announced Monday that it will acquire LivePerson, the veteran digital messaging platform, in an all-stock deal with an enterprise value of approximately $250 million -- a transaction that would unite two of the conversational AI industry's most established players and create one of the largest omnichannel platforms in the space.

The deal, SoundHound's fifth strategic acquisition, carries an equity value of roughly $43 million -- a 22 percent premium over LivePerson's 30-day volume-weighted average share price -- but balloons to $250 million once LivePerson's debt is factored in at a negotiated discount. SoundHound expects to receive $74 million of LivePerson's cash at closing, and the combined company is projected to carry no debt on its balance sheet afterward.

## A Marriage of Voice and Text

The strategic logic is straightforward: SoundHound brings proprietary voice AI technology that powers billions of voice interactions annually across automotive, restaurant, and IoT verticals, while LivePerson's Conversational Cloud platform processes approximately one billion customer messages per month for some of the world's largest enterprises.

"This combination brings together complementary conversational AI pioneers defining the future of agentic customer service," said Keyvan Mohajer, CEO of SoundHound AI, in the announcement.

LivePerson CEO John Sabino framed the deal in terms of shifting consumer expectations. "Artificial boundaries between talking and typing are disappearing, and this merger delivers seamless omnichannel experiences," Sabino said.

The combined entity will boast a formidable enterprise client roster: 25 of the Fortune 100, 12 of the top 15 global banks, four of the top five global airlines, four of the top five global automakers, and more than 10 leading telecommunications providers, operating across over 30 countries.

## The Numbers Behind the Deal

SoundHound is projecting combined 2027 revenue of $350 million to $400 million, with at least $100 million expected to come from LivePerson's existing customer base. Management has also pointed to a longer-term $500 million revenue opportunity from cross-selling voice AI capabilities to LivePerson's digital-first clients and vice versa.

The financial math is notable. SoundHound reported $169 million in revenue for 2025 with a $14 million net loss, while LivePerson is projected to generate $195 million to $205 million in revenue in 2026. If the integration goes smoothly, the combined company could more than double SoundHound's standalone revenue within two years.

But the all-stock structure of the transaction raised immediate concerns on Wall Street about shareholder dilution. The deal includes a price collar mechanism tied to a 10-day volume-weighted average price, with a floor of $7 and a cap of $12 per SoundHound share -- a range that reflects the stock's recent volatility. SOUN shares fell 5.65 percent on the announcement day, closing at $7.85 on trading volume of 54.2 million shares, roughly double the three-month average.

## Market Skepticism Meets Strategic Ambition

The stock market's initial reaction was not unique to SoundHound. Broader AI software names also declined modestly, with C3.ai dropping 1.97 percent and BigBear.ai falling 1.30 percent, reflecting continued caution around AI software valuations. By the following session, however, SOUN shares had recovered modestly, trading up 1.20 percent to $8.42.

The skepticism is understandable. LivePerson, once valued at over $4 billion at its pandemic-era peak, had seen its market capitalization crater to just $40 million before the deal was announced. The company has been through a turbulent period of leadership changes and strategic pivots. For SoundHound, the acquisition is a bet that LivePerson's enterprise relationships and messaging infrastructure remain valuable even as the company itself struggled as a standalone entity.

Constellation Research noted that the deal gives SoundHound immediate access to legacy LivePerson clients for upselling opportunities, along with a treasure trove of customer interaction data that could be used to improve its AI models. The research firm also highlighted the debt retirement at a significant discount as a financially savvy element of the transaction structure.

## What Comes Next

The transaction is expected to close in the second half of 2026, subject to regulatory approvals and a vote by LivePerson's stockholders. Barclays is advising SoundHound, with Latham and Watkins serving as legal counsel. Lazard is advising LivePerson, with Fried Frank as its legal advisor.

SoundHound's upcoming Q1 2026 earnings report, expected on May 7, will give investors their next opportunity to assess the company's standalone trajectory. Analysts are projecting revenue of $42.62 million for the quarter, up sharply from $29.13 million a year earlier, alongside an expected loss of seven cents per share.

For the conversational AI sector more broadly, the deal signals that consolidation is accelerating. As enterprises demand unified platforms that can handle customer interactions seamlessly across voice, chat, and messaging channels, the pressure on point-solution providers to either combine or be left behind is intensifying. SoundHound is placing its bet that bigger -- and broader -- is better.
