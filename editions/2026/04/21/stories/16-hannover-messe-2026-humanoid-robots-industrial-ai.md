# Hannover Messe 2026 Opens With Humanoid Robots and Industrial AI Center Stage

HANNOVER, Germany -- For nearly eight decades, Hannover Messe has served as the world's barometer for industrial technology. This year, the needle swung decisively toward artificial intelligence and the machines it is learning to inhabit.

Germany's flagship industrial trade fair opened Monday under the banner "Think Tech Forward," drawing roughly 2,900 exhibitors from more than 50 countries to a five-day showcase running April 20-24. For the first time in the event's history, humanoid robots and industrial AI claimed center stage -- a symbolic milestone that organizers and attendees alike treated as a declaration: the factory of the future is no longer theoretical.

"Competitiveness is built where innovations are rapidly put into practice," said Jochen Koeckler, CEO of Deutsche Messe, the fair's organizer. "At Hannover Messe, attendees experience firsthand how to deploy AI productively."

## The humanoid moment arrives

Roughly 15 companies dedicated exhibition space to humanoid robotic systems, up from a handful of curiosity-stage demos at previous editions. Agile Robots drew crowds with Agile ONE, a humanoid that perceives its environment, makes autonomous decisions, and operates in real time within complex industrial scenarios. Chinese entrants including Xpeng, Unitree Robotics, PL-Universe Robotics, and Huayan Robotics also displayed working prototypes, underscoring the increasingly global nature of the humanoid race.

The shift from lab to factory floor is accelerating fast. A collaboration between Siemens and NVIDIA has compressed humanoid robot development timelines from 18 months to just seven -- a reduction that, according to Siemens, is "pivotal for transitioning humanoid robots from a concept to a key player in industrial operations." During the fair, Siemens demonstrated tote-handling tasks performed by humanoid systems with what the company called "impressive metrics in performance," enabled in part by private 5G networks that allow real-time data exchanges in dynamic factory conditions.

## Germany bets on compute and AI

The political framing matched the industrial ambition. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz used his opening address to declare that AI is "profoundly reshaping economic activity," particularly in manufacturing, where it enhances productivity, optimizes resources, and reduces costs. Merz announced Germany's goal to double its computing capacity by 2030 compared to 2025 levels, alongside increased investment in microelectronics, quantum computing, and electrification -- a clear signal that Berlin views industrial AI as a matter of national competitiveness.

Brazilian President Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva also spoke at the opening ceremony; Brazil serves as this year's official partner country, reflecting what organizers described as a relationship that "goes beyond trade" into strategic partnership. Former U.S. President Barack Obama contributed a video message.

## A global convergence

Hannover Messe's timing dovetailed with National Robotics Week in the United States (April 4-12), during which NVIDIA spotlighted its "Physical AI" agenda -- the push to bring large-model intelligence into robotic bodies that operate in the real world. NVIDIA unveiled new Isaac GR00T open models that enable robots to understand natural language instructions and perform complex, multistep tasks, as well as Cosmos world models for generating synthetic training data at scale. The convergence of these parallel events on opposite sides of the Atlantic illustrates a broader 2026 theme: industrial manufacturing and AI are no longer adjacent industries but a single, fused domain.

China's presence at the fair reinforced that point. With approximately 700 exhibitors, China was the second-largest national group after host Germany, and AI and robotics companies were its headline delegation -- a marked shift from the hardware-heavy Chinese contingents of years past.

## The new Defense Production Park

In a sign of the times, Hannover Messe also debuted a Defense Production Park featuring around 40 companies, a nod to rising European demand for dual-use technologies that straddle civilian manufacturing and defense applications.

## What it means

More than 3,000 exhibiting companies, a German chancellor pledging to double compute power, humanoid robots shaking hands with visitors on the show floor -- Hannover Messe 2026 is less a trade fair and more a statement of industrial intent. The question is no longer whether AI will reshape manufacturing, but how fast, and who will lead. With development cycles collapsing from years to months and both Western and Chinese firms racing to deploy humanoid workers, the answer may arrive sooner than the factories themselves are ready for it.

*Hannover Messe runs through Friday, April 24.*
