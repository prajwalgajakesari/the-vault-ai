Jeff Bezos' Project Prometheus Nears $10 Billion Raise at $38 Billion Valuation, Backed by BlackRock and JPMorgan

Jeff Bezos is closing a roughly $10 billion round at a $38 billion valuation for Project Prometheus, his physical-AI lab co-led with Vik Bajaj, signaling a second-act bet that AI's next trillion dollars will come from machines that manipulate the physical world, not just tokens on a screen.

# Jeff Bezos' Project Prometheus Nears $10 Billion Raise at $38 Billion Valuation, Backed by BlackRock and JPMorgan

Five years after stepping down as Amazon's chief executive, Jeff Bezos is writing the largest check of his post-Amazon career — and asking Wall Street to match it. According to a Financial Times scoop confirmed Tuesday by Bloomberg, Reuters and The Information, Bezos' stealth AI venture Project Prometheus is in the final stages of closing a roughly **$10 billion funding round at a $38 billion post-money valuation**, with **BlackRock and JPMorgan Asset Management** anchoring a syndicate that also includes sovereign and family-office capital.

The round, if papered as expected in the coming days, would bring Prometheus' total funding to about **$16 billion** — stacking on the **$6.2 billion** the company quietly raised in November 2025 when it emerged from stealth. That makes it one of the three best-capitalized AI companies on earth at launch, behind only OpenAI and Anthropic, and ahead of Elon Musk's xAI at the comparable post-Series-B stage.

## Bezos takes an operational seat

More striking than the dollars is the org chart. Bezos is serving as **co-CEO alongside Vik Bajaj**, the physicist and former Google X / Verily scientist who ran Foresite Labs. It is Bezos' **first operational role at any company since he handed the Amazon CEO job to Andy Jassy in July 2021**, ending a 27-year run as founder-CEO. He is reportedly spending four days a week at Prometheus' new headquarters, with satellite offices in **San Francisco, London and Zurich** already hiring aggressively from DeepMind, Nvidia and Tesla's Optimus team.

"Jeff doesn't do board seats," one investor close to the round told The Information. "When he comes back operational, it means he thinks this is a once-in-a-generation platform shift — and he wants the pen."

## The physical-AI thesis

Prometheus is explicitly **not** building another frontier LLM. Internal materials circulated to LPs describe the company's mission as developing AI systems that **"understand the laws of physics"** — foundation models trained on simulation, sensor, and industrial-process data rather than scraped web text. Target domains include robotics, advanced manufacturing, materials discovery, aerospace and energy.

That puts Prometheus on a direct collision course with a crowded field: **Nvidia's** GR00T robotics stack, humanoid-robot upstarts **Figure** and **1X**, Pittsburgh-based **Skild AI**, and **Physical Intelligence (π)**. What Prometheus brings that none of them can match is Bezos' personal balance sheet plus the implicit promise of demand from Amazon, Blue Origin and Koch Industries — the latter a reported strategic partner.

## A $100 billion holding-company endgame

Multiple outlets, including TechFundingNews and The Decoder, report that the $38 billion lab valuation is only the visible tip of a larger structure. Bezos and Bajaj have pitched investors on a parallel **$100 billion industrial-AI holding company** that would **acquire legacy manufacturing, logistics and energy businesses** and retrofit them with Prometheus' models — a Berkshire-meets-DeepMind roll-up. JPMorgan is said to be advising on the acquisition vehicle separately from its LP commitment to the lab.

"If you believe embodied AI is real, the arbitrage isn't in the model weights — it's in the cash-flowing industrial assets you can re-underwrite with 40% lower opex," said Gavin Baker, managing partner at Atreides Management, on CNBC Tuesday. "Bezos is the one person alive who has actually done that trade at scale, twice."

## Context: the AI capital stack in April 2026

The Prometheus round lands in a market where AI valuations have stopped looking like venture capital and started looking like nation-state finance. **OpenAI** is trading in secondaries at an **$852 billion valuation** following its $122 billion tender. **Anthropic** recently closed at $300 billion-plus. **xAI** sits near $200 billion. A $38 billion mark for a 17-month-old company with no public product would have been unthinkable in 2023; in April 2026 it is, as one BlackRock portfolio manager put it, "priced like a mid-cap utility with optionality."

Notably absent from the cap table: Microsoft, Google and Amazon itself. Bezos has reportedly insisted Prometheus remain **hyperscaler-neutral**, a stance that helped attract BlackRock and JPMorgan, which have grown wary of concentration risk in the OpenAI-Microsoft and Anthropic-Amazon-Google axes.

## What to watch next

Three things. First, the **final term sheet** — Bloomberg reports demand is 2.3x oversubscribed and the round could stretch to $12 billion. Second, the **first model release**: Prometheus has told LPs it will ship a robotics foundation model benchmark by Q4 2026. Third, the **$100 billion holding-company announcement**, expected before year-end, potentially with a first target in industrial automation.

For Bezos, it is the boldest bet since he told shareholders in 1997 that Amazon would lose money for a long, long time.

For the AI industry, it is the clearest signal yet that the next chapter will be written not in chat windows — but in factories, warehouses and on the factory floor of the physical world.
