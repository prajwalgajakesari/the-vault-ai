# States Race Ahead on AI Laws as Idaho, Georgia, and Nebraska Advance Bills

In a surge of bipartisan legislative action, three states are advancing AI safety regulations at critical moments. Idaho and Georgia have sent chatbot safety bills to their governors, while Nebraska faces an April 17 deadline. The flurry reflects a nationwide movement producing over 78 chatbot-specific bills across 27 states.

## Idaho

Idaho approved four AI bills, led by SB 1297, the Conversational AI Safety Act. It requires non-human disclosures within three hours, age verification, parental consent for minors, content restrictions, and automated harm detection.

## Georgia

Georgia's SB 540 notably does not include a carve-out for AI embedded within other services—making it stricter than most state bills. It requires hourly reminders to minor users about the non-human nature of the chatbot. Three of Georgia's seven AI bills have been approved.

## Nebraska

Nebraska's LB 1185 has been attached to the popular Agricultural Data Privacy Act to boost passage chances. The legislature adjourns April 17, creating urgency. Approximately 72% of American teenagers have used AI companion chatbots.

## National Trend

Child safety remains the regulatory consensus—even the Trump administration's executive order exempts child safety from federal preemption attempts. Bills have passed by margins like 68-1, 39-1, and 26-1, showing overwhelming bipartisan support. The global chatbot market is valued at $10-11 billion in 2026.
