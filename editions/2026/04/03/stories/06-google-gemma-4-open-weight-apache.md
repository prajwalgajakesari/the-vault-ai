# Google DeepMind Releases Gemma 4 Open-Weight Models Under Apache 2.0

Google DeepMind has released Gemma 4, a family of four open-weight models built on Gemini 3 research, now available under Apache 2.0. The release encompasses four variants: 2B and 4B edge models, a 26B Mixture-of-Experts, and a 31B dense model for servers. Context windows reach 256K tokens with native vision, audio, and 140+ language support.

The 31B model achieves 89.2% on AIME 2026 math benchmark—up from 20.8% in Gemma 3—and ranks third on Arena AI's text leaderboard. On LiveCodeBench v6, it reaches 80.0% for competitive coding.

"This open-source license provides a foundation for complete developer flexibility and digital sovereignty; granting you complete control over your data, infrastructure and models," Google DeepMind stated.

## Apache 2.0 Licensing Shift

The Apache 2.0 decision removes barriers to enterprise adoption, responding to competitive pressure from Chinese open-weight models like Alibaba's Qwen and Zhipu's GLM-5. Edge models feature 128K context and run offline with near-zero latency on phones, Raspberry Pi, and NVIDIA Jetson.

## Availability

Gemma 4 is available on Hugging Face, Kaggle, Ollama, Google AI Studio, and supports vLLM, llama.cpp, MLX, and LM Studio. AMD provides day-one support for its processors and GPUs. The multimodal capabilities process images and video with variable resolution natively.

## What This Means

Google's Apache 2.0 licensing signals confidence in competing on model quality rather than licensing restrictions—a healthy market dynamic for developers gaining access to world-class AI tools without vendor lock-in.
