# Bipartisan MATCH Act Would Ban AI Chipmaking Equipment Sales to China

A bipartisan group of U.S. lawmakers is moving to dramatically tighten export controls on semiconductor manufacturing equipment destined for China. The Multilateral Alignment of Technology Controls on Hardware (MATCH) Act would prohibit sales and servicing of critical chipmaking tools to five major Chinese firms: SMIC, Hua Hong, Huawei, CXMT, and YMTC.

"China has made it abundantly clear that it intends to dominate the technologies that underpin both our economy and our national defense," said lead sponsor Rep. Michael Baumgartner (R-Wash.).

## Closing the DUV Loophole

The Act specifically targets immersion deep ultraviolet (DUV) lithography systems, closing a significant gap in existing restrictions that only cover the most advanced EUV equipment. China's equipment imports grew from $10.7 billion in 2016 to $51.1 billion in 2024. ASML generated approximately 36% of its net system sales from China in Q4 2025.

The legislation also bans after-sales servicing and maintenance, eliminating a workaround that allowed manufacturers to maintain restricted equipment.

## Global Coordination

The MATCH Act directs U.S. diplomacy toward Japan and the Netherlands to implement matching restrictions, preventing Chinese companies from circumventing U.S. rules through allied nations.

## What to Watch

Industry observers will monitor whether the bill gains traction this session and whether it prompts responses from ASML and allied governments. China's chipmaking industry lags more than five years behind Western manufacturers, and this legislation aims to keep it that way.
