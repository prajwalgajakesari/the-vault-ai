OpenAI Acquires TBPN Tech Talk Show in First-Ever Media Deal

April 3, 2026
4 min read
436 words
3 sources
Key Takeaway
OpenAI acquires TBPN for low hundreds of millions, its first media acquisition, signaling push to control AI narratives.

OpenAI has made its boldest move into mainstream media by acquiring TBPN, the Silicon Valley-favorite tech talk show, marking the AI giant's first-ever acquisition of a media property. The deal, valued in the low hundreds of millions of dollars according to the Financial Times, signals a significant strategic pivot for the company—one that extends its influence well beyond its core AI products and into the realm of public discourse around artificial intelligence itself.

TBPN, which stands for Technology Business Programming Network, is an 11-person operation that hosts a daily three-hour livestream show featuring founders, technologists, and business leaders discussing the latest developments in tech, business, AI, and defense. Since launching in October 2024, the show has cultivated a dedicated following in Silicon Valley circles, averaging about 70,000 viewers per daily episode and projecting roughly $30 million in annual revenue, primarily from advertising.

The show is hosted by Jordi Hays and John Coogan, both former tech founders who have built TBPN into a hub for candid conversations about industry trends. The acquisition places TBPN under the oversight of Chris Lehane, OpenAI's chief political operative, a move that underscores the company's growing focus on shaping public narratives and policy discussions around artificial intelligence.

Despite the acquisition, OpenAI has committed to maintaining editorial independence for the show. Sam Altman, OpenAI's CEO, doubled down on this commitment, stating that "I do not expect TBPN to go any easier on us. Am sure I'll do my part to help enable that with occasional stupid decisions."

OpenAI's acquisition of TBPN represents a significant departure from the company's historical focus on developing and deploying AI technologies. By acquiring a media platform, OpenAI is essentially securing direct access to influence conversations about AI at a moment when public opinion, regulatory scrutiny, and investment decisions are heavily shaped by narratives in tech media.

The timing is particularly noteworthy. As AI regulation continues to tighten globally, controlling a platform that reaches tech founders, investors, and policy influencers is a valuable asset. TBPN's three-hour daily format and live nature make it a platform where nuanced conversations can occur.

The move also signals confidence in TBPN's format and audience. Unlike many traditional media acquisitions, TBPN brings a highly engaged, niche audience rather than mass-market reach. This suggests OpenAI is prioritizing influence over a core demographic rather than seeking to build a mainstream media empire.

The true test will be whether TBPN maintains its editorial credibility and audience trust. If perceived as becoming a mouthpiece for OpenAI, it could quickly lose the audience that makes it valuable. The acquisition also raises questions about how other AI companies might respond.
