Cognichip Emerges From Stealth With $60M to Let AI Design Computer Chips

April 3, 2026
4 min read
265 words
3 sources
Key Takeaway
Cognichip raises $60M Series A for AI chip design, claims 75% cost reduction, Intel CEO joins board.

The semiconductor design industry is notoriously slow, expensive, and complex. Advanced chips can take three to five years from conception to mass production. Now, Cognichip is betting that generative AI can fundamentally reshape this timeline.

On April 1, 2026, Cognichip announced it has raised $60 million in Series A funding, led by Seligman Ventures, to deploy what the company calls Artificial Chip Intelligence (ACI): a physics-informed AI system designed to rethink chip design. The round brings total funding to $93 million since its 2024 founding.

Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan will join Cognichip's board. "The semiconductor industry is at a critical juncture; an AI framework for innovation and efficiency will unlock massive global opportunity," Tan said.

Cognichip claims its platform can reduce the effort required for chip design by up to 50% and cut overall cost by more than 75%. The company builds physics-informed AI models trained on semiconductor-specific data, understanding fundamental physics rather than just statistical patterns.

"The next wave of progress to significantly reduce chip design cycles will not come from incremental optimization of existing design tools, but from using AI to parallelize what has historically been a highly serial chip design process," said Umesh Padval of Seligman Ventures.

Cognichip is working with more than 30 semiconductor design companies. Competitors ChipAgents ($74M extended Series A) and Ricursive ($300M Series A) underscore venture capital conviction that AI-driven chip design is a multi-billion-dollar opportunity.

The real test comes when a new chip designed using Cognichip's platform reaches production. Intel CEO's board participation signals credibility, but execution will determine whether the 75% cost reduction claims hold up.
