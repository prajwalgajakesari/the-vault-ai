DeepSeek Launches V4, a Trillion-Parameter Open-Weight MoE Model for $5.2 Million

April 3, 2026
4 min read
193 words
3 sources
Key Takeaway
DeepSeek V4 trains for $5.2M with 1T parameters and 1M context window, challenging Western AI dominance.

DeepSeek has entered the trillion-parameter era with V4—an ambitious Mixture-of-Experts model with approximately 1 trillion total parameters but only 37 billion active per token. Trained for an estimated $5.2 million, V4 challenges fundamental assumptions about the cost of frontier AI.

The model features a 1-million-token context window powered by Engram Conditional Memory, native multimodal generation across text, image, video, and audio, and API pricing at $0.30 per million input tokens—roughly 8x cheaper than GPT-4o and 50x cheaper than Claude Opus.

Three core innovations enable V4's efficiency: Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections solve training stability at trillion-parameter scale, Engram Conditional Memory powers the extraordinary context window with 97% accuracy on needle-in-haystack retrieval, and Sparse Attention with Lightning Indexer enables rapid long-context processing.

DeepSeek partnered with Huawei and Cambricon to optimize V4 for domestic Chinese chips, validating that the Chinese semiconductor stack can run frontier models. The model will be released under Apache 2.0 license—a geopolitical statement about AI development's future direction.

V4 accelerates the decoupling of frontier AI from Western corporate control. China's hardware constraints pushed efficiency innovations that benefit the broader field. For Western AI companies, V4 intensifies competitive pressure on both pricing and open-source strategy.
