# Anthropic Forms AnthroPAC, an Employee-Funded Bipartisan Political Action Committee

Anthropic has officially launched AnthroPAC, a new employee-funded political action committee, joining major technology firms in seeking to influence federal elections and AI policy. Filed with the Federal Election Commission on Friday, AnthroPAC arrives at a particularly fraught moment as the AI sector grapples with military use, export controls, and safety standards.

The committee will be funded exclusively by voluntary employee contributions, capped at $5,000 per person per year under federal law. According to filing materials, it "will donate to candidates from both parties" and will be overseen by a bipartisan board. Treasurer is Allison Rossi of Anthropic.

## Pentagon Context

The timing is no accident. Anthropic has been at odds with the Trump administration over Pentagon contracts and AI safety policies. In February, the company faced a Pentagon ultimatum to loosen safety policies or lose federal contracts. Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth designated Anthropic a national security "supply-chain risk." Anthropic sued, arguing the designation was retaliatory.

## Midterm Strategy

AnthroPAC explicitly aims to support lawmakers and candidates in the November 2026 midterm elections. With Congressional control potentially in play, Anthropic seeks to elect candidates sympathetic to AI safety regulation—positioning that clashes with the administration's push for unconstrained AI scaling.

## What This Means

AnthroPAC signals that Anthropic views political engagement as necessary to defend its interests. The company has decided that aggressive litigation alone is insufficient—it needs sophisticated political engagement as well.
