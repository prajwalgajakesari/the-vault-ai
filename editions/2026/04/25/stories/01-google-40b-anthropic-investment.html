# Google Plans to Invest Up to $40 Billion in Anthropic in Cash and Compute

Alphabet, Google's parent company, is pouring up to $40 billion into Anthropic in what amounts to the single largest corporate investment in a privately held artificial-intelligence company. The deal, announced on April 24, 2026, pairs a $10 billion immediate cash injection at a $350 billion valuation with an additional $30 billion contingent on Anthropic meeting undisclosed performance targets -- underscoring just how aggressively Big Tech is betting that the AI race will be won on infrastructure as much as on algorithms.

The Deal

Under the terms of the agreement, Google will commit $10 billion up front to Anthropic PBC, matching the $350 billion valuation set in the startup's February 2026 funding round. If Anthropic hits certain operational and performance milestones, Google will deploy an additional $30 billion over time. Critically, the deal is not cash alone: Google Cloud will provision 5 gigawatts of computing capacity to Anthropic over the next five years, giving the Claude maker access to a massive fleet of Google's custom Tensor Processing Units (TPUs).

Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei framed the partnership as a response to explosive demand. "Our users tell us Claude is increasingly essential to how they work, and we need to build the infrastructure to keep pace with rapidly growing demand," Amodei said in a statement accompanying the announcement.

Google Cloud CEO Thomas Kurian, meanwhile, positioned the investment as part of a broader platform strategy. "Anthropic's choice to significantly expand its usage of TPUs reflects the strong price-performance and efficiency its teams have seen with TPUs for several years," Kurian said, noting that Google can monetize AI through chips, models, agent platforms, subscriptions, and advertising.

An Arms Race in Capital

The deal lands just days after Amazon confirmed it will invest up to $25 billion in Anthropic -- $5 billion immediately at the same $350 billion valuation, with an option for $20 billion more over time. Combined, the two Big Tech backers have now pledged as much as $65 billion to a single AI startup, a figure that would have seemed fantastical even 18 months ago.

Anthropic's financial trajectory helps explain the frenzy. The company's annualized revenue run rate surpassed $30 billion in April 2026, up from roughly $9 billion at the end of 2025 -- a more-than-threefold increase in about four months. Anthropic now counts more than 1,000 enterprise clients paying over $1 million annually, a figure that has more than doubled in recent months. That growth has in some estimates pushed Anthropic past OpenAI, which was generating an estimated $25 billion annualized revenue earlier this year.

The capital will be directed primarily at compute infrastructure. Anthropic has also separately sealed a deal with Broadcom to access about 3.5 gigawatts of computing power through Google-designed TPU chips -- a sign that the company is building redundancy and scale into its supply chain ahead of what it expects will be continued exponential demand for Claude.

Why This Matters

The sheer scale of these investments signals a structural shift in how the AI industry is being financed. Rather than traditional venture rounds measured in hundreds of millions, the frontier AI labs are now absorbing capital at a pace once reserved for sovereign infrastructure projects. Google's $40 billion commitment alone is larger than the GDP of more than 80 countries.

For Google, the deal represents a calculated hedge. The company continues to develop its own Gemini family of models internally, but by backing Anthropic it ensures that even if a competitor's model pulls ahead, Google Cloud's infrastructure remains central to the AI ecosystem. It is a bet on picks and shovels as much as on any particular prospector.

For the broader market, the Google and Amazon investments raise pointed questions about concentration. Two hyperscalers now hold enormous financial leverage over Anthropic, a company founded in 2021 by former OpenAI researchers specifically to pursue AI safety. Antitrust regulators in Washington and Brussels have already begun scrutinizing whether these deals effectively give Big Tech control over nominally independent startups without triggering traditional merger review thresholds.

What to Watch Next

All eyes now turn to whether Anthropic will pursue an initial public offering later this year. The company has previously signaled a potential IPO in Q4 2026, and a $350 billion private valuation would make it one of the largest technology debuts in history. The performance targets embedded in the Google deal -- still undisclosed -- will also be closely watched, as they could shape Anthropic's product roadmap and the cadence of its model releases. Meanwhile, OpenAI and xAI are both expected to announce their own mega-rounds in the coming months, suggesting the capital arms race in AI is far from over.
