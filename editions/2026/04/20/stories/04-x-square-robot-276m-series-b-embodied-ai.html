X Square Robot Raises $276 Million Series B as China Doubles Down on Embodied AI

X Square Robot's $276M Series B, led by Xiaomi and Sequoia China just weeks after a $140M round from ByteDance, signals that China's embodied AI race is consolidating around a handful of well-capitalized contenders ready to challenge US humanoid leaders.

# X Square Robot Raises $276 Million Series B as China Doubles Down on Embodied AI

Beijing-based X Square Robot has closed a Series B funding round of nearly $276 million led by Xiaomi and Sequoia China, according to reports this week from Pandaily and Caixin Global — capping a whirlwind two-month fundraising sprint that has vaulted the two-year-old startup into the top tier of China's embodied AI ecosystem.

The round arrives barely six weeks after X Square sealed a $140 million Series A++ led by ByteDance and existing investor Sequoia China in January, bringing total capital raised to roughly $416 million since the company emerged from stealth. Pitchbook data pegs the post-money valuation in the high single-digit billions of yuan, a figure that would place X Square alongside Unitree Robotics and Agibot as the most richly valued pure-play humanoid companies in the country.

"Embodied intelligence is the next computing platform, and we believe X Square's foundation-model-first approach is the fastest path to robots that can actually be useful outside a lab," said a Xiaomi Corporate Venture Capital spokesperson quoted by Caixin. Xiaomi — which has been quietly accumulating stakes across the robotics stack to feed its own CyberOne humanoid program — is taking a board observer seat as part of the deal.

## A foundation-model-first bet

X Square was founded in 2023 by a team of roughly 20 researchers drawn from Baidu's autonomous-driving unit, Tsinghua University, and Stanford's robotics lab. Unlike rivals that began with hardware and layered software on top, X Square spent its first 18 months training what it calls a "Wall-A" embodied foundation model — a vision-language-action network designed to transfer skills across different robot bodies.

"We don't think the winner of this race will be the company with the most elegant actuator," co-founder and CEO Wang He told Pandaily earlier this year. "It will be the company whose model can walk into an unfamiliar environment and still do the job. That is fundamentally an AI problem, not a mechatronics problem."

The company has so far unveiled two bipedal platforms and a wheeled manipulator, all sharing the same underlying model. Early pilots, according to Caixin, are running in three verticals: tutoring robots in private education centers in Shenzhen, concierge and luggage-handling units at a Hilton-branded property in Shanghai, and companion robots being trialed with elder-care operators in Hangzhou. A newly announced commercial partnership with classifieds giant 58.com is expected to route X Square's machines into home-services workflows — cleaning, moving, and minor maintenance jobs booked through 58's consumer app.

## China's embodied AI surge

The raise lands in the middle of what bankers in Shanghai are calling "humanoid summer." Chinese embodied AI startups have collectively raised north of $4 billion since the start of 2026, with Unitree, Agibot, Galbot, and LimX Dynamics all closing nine-figure rounds. Beijing's 2025 industrial policy designating humanoid robots a "strategic emerging industry" has unlocked provincial matching funds, and state-backed LPs are quietly anchoring many of these rounds.

The contrast with the US ecosystem is sharpening. Figure, 1X Technologies, and Agility Robotics dominate Western headlines, but their combined 2025 funding trails the Chinese cohort's 2026 pace — and none benefit from the vertically integrated supply chain advantage Chinese players draw from Shenzhen's hardware clusters.

"The bottleneck in humanoid robotics isn't hardware anymore — it's the foundation model that lets a machine generalize from one kitchen to another," said Ming-Hsuan Yang, a robotics professor at UC Merced who tracks the sector. "That's where China's compute and data advantages start to matter. Companies like X Square are essentially running the OpenAI playbook on robot bodies."

## Xiaomi's robotics ambitions

For Xiaomi, the investment is strategic as much as financial. The smartphone-turned-EV conglomerate has been publicly vague about its humanoid roadmap, but insiders say CEO Lei Jun views robotics as the natural extension of Xiaomi's auto and smart-home ecosystems — sharing batteries, actuators, and the HyperOS software layer. Backing X Square gives Xiaomi optionality on a best-in-class model partner without committing to building one in-house.

Sequoia China, which now has participated in three consecutive X Square rounds, described the follow-on as "unusually high conviction" in a note circulated to LPs this week. Other participants in the Series B reportedly include Hongshan, Meituan's strategic fund, and a sovereign vehicle tied to the Beijing municipal government.

## What to watch

Three questions will shape X Square's next twelve months. First, can the 58.com and hospitality pilots scale from dozens of units to thousands — the threshold where unit economics start to work? Second, how deeply will Xiaomi's synergies go? Shared supply chains could cut X Square's bill of materials by an estimated 30%, but deeper integration risks locking the startup into a single customer. And third, with Chinese humanoid peers openly discussing Hong Kong or Shanghai STAR Market listings for 2027, will X Square — now arguably China's most talked-about robotics unicorn — move up its own IPO timetable?

For a company that did not exist three years ago, those are enviable problems to have.
