ElliQ Becomes First AI Social Robot to Receive Full Medicaid Coverage

April 4, 20264 min read847 words

Key Takeaway

Washington State becomes the first to cover an AI social robot through Medicaid, signaling potential for widespread AI in elderly care.

Intuition Robotics has achieved a historic milestone: ElliQ, its AI-powered social robot, is now the first device of its kind to receive full Medicaid reimbursement coverage. Washington State authorized a statewide reimbursement code for the companion robot, making it available to eligible beneficiaries in their home care services.

Our mission is to empower older adults with technology that fosters well-being and connection. Washington's leadership in making ElliQ available through Medicaid sets a national precedent, said Dor Skuler, CEO and Co-Founder of Intuition Robotics.

"Our mission is to empower older adults with technology that fosters well-being and connection. Washington's leadership in making ElliQ available through Medicaid sets a national precedent."

— Dor Skuler

Pilot results show participants engaged with ElliQ an average of 60 times per day. Ninety-five percent reported decreased isolation, 100% found it useful for improving wellness, and 43% reported feeling safer and less anxious. Washington authorized coverage with no enrollment cap.

ElliQ is a voice-operated, AI-enabled companion that initiates conversations, offers medication reminders, suggests wellness activities, and performs daily health check-ins. It complements rather than replaces human care services.

The coverage applies to Medicaid recipients enrolled in Community First Choice and Roads to Community Living programs. The company has also secured partnerships with Medicare Advantage plans, indicating a two-track public-private strategy.
