## LLMs & GenAI

### Anthropic's 'Mythos' Model Leaked — Called a 'Step Change' in Capabilities
Anthropic accidentally exposed draft blog post materials in an unsecured public data lake, revealing the existence of a new model codenamed "Mythos" that has been internally described as a "step change" in performance. The leak included nearly 3,000 unpublished assets linked to Anthropic's blog infrastructure. While the company has since acknowledged that it is testing the Mythos model, Anthropic has declined to provide a timeline for public release.

**Source:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-says-testing-mythos-powerful-new-ai-model-after-data-leak-reveals-its-existence-step-change-in-capabilities/)

### GPT-5.4 Drops With Million-Token Context and Three Variants
OpenAI launched GPT-5.4 on March 5 in three distinct flavors designed to address different use cases and requirements. The Standard variant provides broad capability for general tasks, the Thinking model prioritizes reasoning-first approaches, and the Pro tier delivers maximum capability for enterprise workloads. The headline feature is a 1-million-token context window that enables processing of entire books or extensive codebases in a single interaction.

**Source:** [LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)

### Google Ships Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite for Speed Junkies
Google introduced Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, an efficiency-focused model designed for applications where latency matters more than raw capability. The model delivers 2.5x faster response times and 45% faster output generation compared to earlier Gemini versions, making it an ideal choice for real-time applications, edge deployment, and cost-sensitive workloads.

**Source:** [LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)

### Alibaba's Qwen 3.5 9B Punches Way Above Its Weight
Alibaba's Qwen team released the Qwen 3.5 Small Model Series, featuring a 9-billion-parameter model that scored 81.7 on GPQA Diamond — outperforming OpenAI's gpt-oss-120B, a model thirteen times its size. The result challenges the prevailing assumption that parameter count directly determines capability and demonstrates that efficient architecture and training methodology can achieve outsized performance on challenging reasoning benchmarks.

**Source:** [Build Fast with AI](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-models-march-2026-releases)

### Apple's AI-Powered Siri Overhaul Targets March Release With iOS 26.4
Apple is rolling out a completely reimagined Siri as a context-aware assistant featuring on-screen awareness and seamless cross-app integration, powered by Google's Gemini model running on Apple's Private Cloud Compute infrastructure. The update represents a fundamental departure from Siri's traditional voice-command interface toward a more intelligent, visually-aware system capable of understanding what's displayed on screen.

**Source:** [Vocal Media](https://vocal.media/futurism/apple-s-power-move-everything-announced-in-the-massive-march-2026-refresh)

## Research

### 'The AI Scientist' Becomes First Fully AI-Generated Paper Published in Nature
A paper describing an AI system capable of autonomously conducting scientific research—from conceiving novel ideas through experimental design to writing and formatting formal manuscripts—has been published in Nature, marking the first time a fully AI-generated paper has survived the rigorous peer-review process at that prestigious level. The research was developed by teams at Sakana AI, the University of British Columbia, the Vector Institute, and Oxford University.

**Source:** [StartupHub.ai](https://www.startuphub.ai/ai-news/technology/2026/ai-scientist-paper-published-in-nature)

### NVIDIA Drops Nemotron 3 Super: 120B Coding Beast Scores 60% on SWE-Bench
NVIDIA quietly released Nemotron 3 Super during GTC—a 120-billion-parameter enterprise coding model that achieved 60.47% on SWE-Bench Verified, placing it in the upper tier of production-grade coding models. The release positions NVIDIA as a serious player in the model layer, moving beyond its traditional dominance in chips and infrastructure toward building frontier capabilities.

**Source:** [ClawPod](https://www.clawpod.co/blog/llm-news-open-source-ai-models-march-2026)

### Physics-Informed ML Algorithm Breaks New Ground in Climate Modeling
Researchers at the University of Hawai'i at Mānoa unveiled an algorithm that constrains AI systems to obey the laws of physics while processing complex scientific datasets, resulting in dramatically more accurate predictions for fluid dynamics and climate modeling. The breakthrough represents a meaningful step toward trustworthy scientific AI by ensuring that models cannot produce physically impossible outputs.

**Source:** [Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)

### ABB and NVIDIA Close the Simulation-to-Reality Gap in Robotics
ABB Robotics and NVIDIA integrated Omniverse simulation libraries into ABB's RobotStudio platform, achieving up to 99% correlation between simulated and real-world robot behavior. By eliminating the need for extensive physical prototypes, manufacturers can cut commissioning times by 80% and reduce associated costs by 40%.

**Source:** [Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)

Anthropic is closing in at $19 billion in annualized revenue, creating genuine competition at the frontier. These figures move AI beyond speculation and into the territory of real, enterprise-scale businesses.

**Source:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-says-testing-mythos-powerful-new-ai-model-after-data-leak-reveals-its-existence-step-change-in-capabilities/)

### Meta Cuts 700 Jobs, Redirects $135B to AI Data Centers
Meta is executing a dramatic strategic pivot—eliminating 700 positions while committing $135 billion to AI data center infrastructure. The company simultaneously launched Meta Small Business, a platform targeting 250 million small and medium-sized businesses globally with AI-powered tools and services. The restructuring signals Meta's conviction that its future is infrastructure, not headcount.

**Source:** [Digital Applied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/march-2026-ai-roundup-month-that-changed-everything)

### Yann LeCun's AMI Labs Raises $1.03B — Largest European Seed Round Ever
AMI Labs, backed by Bezos, NVIDIA, Samsung, and Temasek, closed a $1.03 billion seed round—the largest ever raised by a European AI company at the seed stage. The company is being built on the JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) framework pioneered by Yann LeCun, representing the most well-capitalized bet against the autoregressive text-prediction paradigm that powers ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.

**Source:** [AI Funding Tracker](https://aifundingtracker.com/ai-startup-funding-news-today/)

### Robotics Enters Its Mega-Round Era: $1.2B Raised in One Week
Mind Robotics, Rhoda AI, Sunday, and Oxa collectively raised over $1.2 billion in a single week, signaling that robotics has definitively entered the mega-funding era. Robotics is no longer a niche venture category but rather an emerging competitor for capital alongside pure-play AI model companies. The acceleration reflects both a maturing market and technological breakthroughs that have made practical robotics solutions viable.

**Source:** [AI Funding Tracker](https://aifundingtracker.com/ai-startup-funding-news-today/)

Adobe and NVIDIA announced a strategic partnership to deliver the next generation of Firefly foundational models and build agentic creative workflows that integrate NVIDIA's compute infrastructure with Adobe's market-leading creative tools. The disconnect between federal intent and state action reflects deeper tensions about how to govern rapidly evolving technology.

**Source:** [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/28/nx-s1-5755062/trump-wants-a-deadlocked-congress-to-move-on-ai-frustrated-states-say-they-already-have)
