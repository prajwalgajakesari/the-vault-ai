Yann LeCun's AMI Labs Raises $1.03B — Largest European Seed Round Ever

AI Funding Tracker March 28, 2026

AMI Labs, backed by Bezos, NVIDIA, Samsung, and Temasek, closed a $1.03 billion seed round—the largest ever raised by a European AI company at the seed stage. The company is being built on the JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) framework pioneered by Yann LeCun, representing the most well-capitalized bet against the autoregressive text-prediction paradigm that powers ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. LeCun's vision of world models and unsupervised learning stands in philosophical contrast to the current dominant approach, and the funding validates that serious institutional investors see alternative architectures as strategically important. The scale of the round signals that Europe is aggressively backing AI infrastructure and models to reduce dependence on US-based providers, while simultaneously validating LeCun's influence and the market appetite for novel approaches to foundation models.
