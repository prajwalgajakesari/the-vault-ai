Apple Will Let Gemini and Claude Replace ChatGPT Inside Siri With iOS 27

The Vault — AI Edition · March 29, 2026
Estimated reading time: 5 minutes

Apple is about to hand its most important interface—the voice assistant billions of users rely on—to anyone who wants to compete for it.

Bloomberg's Mark Gurman reported that iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 will introduce a new Extensions system that fundamentally changes how Siri works. Instead of defaulting to OpenAI's ChatGPT for AI tasks, users will be able to download AI chatbot apps and integrate them directly into Siri. Gemini, Claude, and others will sit alongside ChatGPT as options.

This is the most significant architectural shift in Siri's history. Apple is positioning itself as a platform layer for AI, not a model layer. The distinction matters: Apple doesn't care which AI model answers your question, as long as the question flows through Apple hardware.

The move arrives at a delicate moment. OpenAI signed an exclusive deal with Apple in 2024 to power Siri's ChatGPT integration. That deal didn't contemplate Extensions. Now Apple is opening the door just wide enough for competitors to slip through. OpenAI's exclusivity just became non-exclusive—at least for users willing to download an app and change settings.

Expect chaos for approximately 90 days, then consolidation. Power users will gravitate toward Claude and Gemini, which have stronger reasoning capabilities than ChatGPT in many domains. Casual users will stick with whatever comes pre-selected. Apple will watch usage patterns obsessively and optimize which model gets recommended by default.

The feature launches at WWDC on June 8, currently in testing. Developers who've seen it report that integrating a third-party model into Siri requires substantial backend work to handle request routing, context management, and response formatting. It's not trivial. But it's possible.

What Apple gains: plausible deniability if any AI assistant makes a mistake, says something offensive, or gives bad advice. Apple's name appears nowhere in the interaction. The liability belongs to the model provider.

What Apple loses: the ability to claim Siri is an integrated, coherent product. Siri becomes a routing layer, nothing more. The experience quality depends entirely on whoever built the model answering your question.

The timing matters too. Google just released Gemini 3.1, which finally has capabilities that justify replacing ChatGPT for many tasks. Anthropic's Claude continues its march upmarket, stealing work from GPT-4. Apple's opening this door now because closing it later would mean telling users they can't use better AI. That's a losing argument.

One more thing: this signals Apple's confidence that it won't develop a world-class foundational model in-house. Rather than invest another $10 billion to compete with OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic, Apple is accepting that commodity AI is fine. Let competitors fight over model quality. Apple will own the interface.

Announcement comes June 8 at WWDC. Availability likely falls in September alongside new iPhones. By year-end 2026, most iPhone users will have seen the Extensions UI at least once. By Q1 2027, Claude and Gemini will have measurable share of Siri requests.

OpenAI's exclusivity lasted 18 months. That's not nothing. It's also not forever.
