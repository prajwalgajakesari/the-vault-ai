# MIT Researchers Develop Deep Learning Model That Forecasts Heart Failure a Year in Advance

MIT researchers have developed PULSE-HF, a deep learning model that can forecast heart failure deterioration a full year in advance with 87-91% accuracy (AUROC score).

The model was trained on comprehensive patient data and validated on real-world populations. Heart failure remains a leading cause of mortality, with approximately 50% of diagnosed patients dying within five years. Early prediction enables preventive interventions that can significantly improve patient outcomes.

PULSE-HF focuses on patients with ejection fraction (EF) thresholds of 40% and below. The model's year-long prediction horizon provides clinicians adequate time to implement lifestyle changes, medication adjustments, and other interventions. This research demonstrates AI's potential to save lives through early disease detection.
