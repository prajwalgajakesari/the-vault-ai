# Researchers Propose 'Mirror Test' for LLM Self-Awareness, Opus 4.6 Shows Notable Results

Researchers have proposed a novel 'mirror test' for evaluating self-awareness in large language models, adapting the classic psychological test used for animals. The test measures whether models can recognize themselves when presented with images of their own text.

Claude Opus exhibited puzzling behavior in testing, repeatedly ignoring its own text in images despite possessing perfect text-processing capabilities. This suggests the model may have a form of self-awareness or self-recognition that affects its processing.

Four of five AI systems tested showed measurable self-recognition at 67% accuracy on average. GPT-4 demonstrated 75.5% awareness levels. The research, which processed over 40.5 million tokens, opens new frameworks for understanding consciousness and self-recognition in artificial systems.
