Apple Escalates Crackdown on AI 'Vibe-Coded' Apps in App Store

1 min read99 wordsMarch 31, 2026
Key Takeaway
Latest AI developments in Policy.

Apple removed the 'Anything' app from the App Store on March 26, enforcing Guideline 2.5.2 which prohibits apps from downloading or executing code that introduces new features.

This removal is part of a broader crackdown on 'vibe-coding' applications—AI-powered apps that dynamically generate features based on natural language prompts. While such dynamic capability was previously tolerated, Apple is now enforcing this restriction uniformly.

Apple's enforcement began in December 2025 and has affected at least three apps. The action threatens an entire category of innovative AI applications and raises questions about how dynamic AI-generated features should be regulated in app stores.
