# White House Unveils National AI Policy Framework, Calls for Federal Preemption of State Laws

The White House has unveiled a comprehensive seven-pillar national AI policy framework released on March 20, 2026, emphasizing federal preemption of conflicting state AI laws.

The framework prioritizes American innovation and competitiveness while establishing consistent standards across states. Rather than creating new regulatory agencies, the approach leverages existing federal structures to oversee AI development and deployment.

The administration argues that a patchwork of conflicting state laws would undermine American AI innovation and global competitiveness. The framework focuses on enabling responsible AI development while maintaining America's technological leadership. This represents a significant shift toward unified federal AI governance.
