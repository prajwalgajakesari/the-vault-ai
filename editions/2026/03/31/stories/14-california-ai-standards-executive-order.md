# California Imposes New AI Standards for State Vendors via Executive Order

Governor Gavin Newsom signed Executive Order N-5-26 on March 30, mandating new AI standards for companies seeking to contract with the State of California.

The executive order requires AI vendors to certify that their systems include safeguards across three key compliance areas. Companies have 120 days to implement the required changes before their contracts can be renewed or expanded.

This represents one of the first concrete uses of executive authority to enforce AI safety standards in government procurement. The order signals California's commitment to responsible AI deployment in public sector operations and may influence corporate AI governance practices nationwide.
