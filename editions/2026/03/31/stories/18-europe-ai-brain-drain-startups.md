# Europe's AI Sector Faces Widening Brain Drain as 56% of Startups Eye Relocation

Europe's AI sector faces a widening brain drain as 56% of AI startups report considering relocation to the United States, primarily driven by significant salary and funding disparities.

A senior machine learning engineer at a top European AI lab can expect compensation of 120,000-180,000, while US counterparts command $350,000-$700,000—nearly 4x higher. This talent differential directly impacts startup competitiveness and innovation capacity.

The funding landscape exacerbates the problem. The US deployed $119.8 billion in late-stage VC capital compared to Europe's $13.1 billion—a 9x gap. Unicorn formation rates reflect this disparity: 4.8% of US startups achieve unicorn status versus 1.5% in Europe. Despite record EU funding, structural economic advantages continue pushing talent and capital toward the United States.
