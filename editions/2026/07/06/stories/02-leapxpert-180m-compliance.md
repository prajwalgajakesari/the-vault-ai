# LeapXpert Raises $180M to Bring AI Compliance Monitoring to Enterprise Messaging

For years, the most consequential business conversations have been happening in the one place enterprises could not see: the group chats, direct messages and back-channels of WhatsApp, iMessage, Signal and WeChat. On June 30, 2026, LeapXpert closed a $180 million growth round to change that, betting that the same governed message data compliance teams once treated as a liability is about to become one of the most valuable assets a regulated company owns.

The round, the largest in the New York-based company's seven-year history, was led by Riverwood Capital, with existing backer Portage Ventures also participating. It dwarfs the $22 million LeapXpert raised in early 2023 to monitor employee chats for compliance, and it signals how sharply the market has pivoted from simply archiving off-channel messages to mining them.

## From compliance headache to intelligence layer

LeapXpert, founded in 2017 by Dima Gutzeit and Avi Pardo, built its business closing a gap that has cost the financial industry dearly. Regulators including the SEC and CFTC have levied billions of dollars in fines over unmonitored "off-channel" communications, forcing banks to either ban consumer messaging apps outright or find a way to capture them. LeapXpert's platform lets employees keep using the channels clients prefer while making every message timestamped, immutable and exportable into archiving, surveillance and eDiscovery systems.

That was the old pitch. The new one, reflected in the company's rebrand around what it calls "Governed Communication Intelligence," is that captured conversations are a data goldmine once AI is layered on top.

"Messaging is where business happens now," said Dima Gutzeit, Founder and CEO of LeapXpert. "The next wave of enterprise value will come from making those conversations trusted, connected, and actionable. But AI can only work with what enterprises can see and govern. Our mission is to give every organization the infrastructure to govern their conversations and the intelligence to act on them."

Speaking to UC Today after the raise, Gutzeit framed the shift as one he watched build across two decades in enterprise software. "I spent more than twenty years building enterprise systems and the whole time the enterprise conversation was about email, the infrastructure, the governance, the data," he said. "But the real conversations had quietly moved somewhere else. The deals, the relationships, the decisions were happening on WhatsApp and iMessage, and the enterprise had no idea. That gap is why we exist."

## Why the AI agents need governance first

The strategic logic behind the round is timing. Gutzeit argues that AI agents are about to move directly inside consumer messaging channels, working alongside human employees in live client conversations. That, he says, only works if the governance layer already exists.

"What's coming next is the part people are only starting to see: AI agents are going to live right inside those channels, working alongside people in the conversations that matter most," Gutzeit told UC Today. "That only works if every one of those conversations is governed and owned from the moment it starts. You can't bolt that on afterward, governance has to be there from the first message. That's what we've spent seven years building."

Co-founder and Chief Business Officer Avi Pardo describes the downstream payoff as vertical-specific but universal in value. "Once the conversations are governed and owned, the interesting part starts. That data feeds everything, it triggers the follow-up, routes the request to the right team, kicks off the workflow, drops the context into your CRM and your AI agents," Pardo said. "A bank surfaces conduct risk, an agency answers a FOIA request, a manufacturer never loses the client relationship when a rep walks out the door. The use case is different every time. The data underneath it is valuable every time."

## The numbers and the backers

LeapXpert says hundreds of organizations rely on its platform today, spanning leading financial institutions, government agencies and global enterprises. The company was named a Visionary in the Gartner Magic Quadrant for Digital Communications Governance and Archiving in each of the past two years, made the Deloitte Technology Fast 500 in both 2024 and 2025, and joined the Financial Times' 2026 list of the fastest-growing companies in America.

Riverwood Capital, which manages over $6 billion and focuses on high-growth technology, is taking a board seat. "The first generation of enterprise communication software archived conversations. The next governed them. The latest uses AI to unlock value from every interaction," said Jeff Parks, Co-Founder and Managing Partner of Riverwood Capital, who is joining LeapXpert's board. "LeapXpert leads that progression today, and no one is better positioned for what comes next."

Ricky Lai, General Partner at Portage Ventures, said the company "sits at the center of one of the largest untapped sources of enterprise intelligence: trusted, governed customer conversations." Houlihan Lokey advised LeapXpert on the deal, with Cooley LLP as legal counsel; Foley & Lardner LLP represented Riverwood.

The company plans to use the proceeds to deepen the platform's ability to understand and act on governed conversations, accelerate growth across financial services, the public sector and the broader enterprise market, and expand its senior leadership team. Financial services was the proving ground; Pardo says government has become the fastest-growing segment, with the Forbes Global 2000 the intended third wave.

## Analysis: surveillance, or intelligence?

LeapXpert's rebrand captures a broader tension in regulated-industry technology. What compliance officers frame as necessary recordkeeping, employees may experience as pervasive surveillance of channels they consider personal. The company's answer is architectural: it governs a distinct professional identity on consumer apps rather than harvesting an employee's private messages. Still, as AI moves from passively indexing chats to actively summarizing conduct risk and scoring conversations, the line between oversight and monitoring will grow harder to draw, and privacy regulators outside the US may scrutinize it more aggressively than financial regulators have.

The bigger bet is that "communication intelligence" becomes a category rather than a feature. Rivals such as Theta Lake and incumbent archiving vendors are racing toward similar AI-governance suites, and hyperscalers embedding agents into their own collaboration tools could compress the market. LeapXpert's advantage is that it already sits on the consumer channels its competitors struggle to reach.

## What to watch next

Watch whether LeapXpert can convert government momentum and Forbes Global 2000 interest into disclosed customer counts and revenue milestones, the metrics that would justify a round this size. Watch, too, for how quickly the AI agents Gutzeit describes actually ship inside WhatsApp and iMessage, and whether Apple and Meta's own platform rules accommodate them. And watch the regulators: the same off-channel enforcement that built LeapXpert's business could, if privacy rules tighten, reshape how far AI is allowed to read into the conversations it now governs.
