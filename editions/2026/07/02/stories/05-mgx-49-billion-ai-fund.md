# Abu Dhabi's MGX Raises $49 Billion for One of the Largest AI Funds Ever

Abu Dhabi's MGX said on Wednesday that it had closed its first dedicated fund at $49 billion in total commitments, exceeding a $45 billion target and assembling one of the single largest pools of capital ever directed at artificial intelligence. In roughly two years, a firm that did not exist before 2024 has vaulted into the top tier of AI financiers, a position that until recently belonged almost exclusively to Silicon Valley venture giants and a handful of American mega-caps.

The size alone reframes the map of AI capital. MGX Fund I is larger than most venture funds in history and rivals the biggest technology-focused vehicles ever raised, including SoftBank's original $100 billion Vision Fund in scale of ambition if not yet in total assets. MGX has said it is targeting $100 billion in assets under management over time, which means this close represents roughly the halfway point of a far larger build-out.

## The Backers Behind the Fund

MGX was established in 2024 as a private investment firm owned by two of Abu Dhabi's most powerful institutions: Mubadala Investment Company, the emirate's roughly $300 billion sovereign wealth fund, and G42, the AI and cloud-computing group. It is chaired by Sheikh Tahnoon bin Zayed Al Nahyan, Deputy Ruler of Abu Dhabi and the UAE's national security adviser, a figure who sits at the center of the emirate's overlapping investment, technology and intelligence apparatus.

The names of the outside investors in Fund I were not disclosed. MGX said the vehicle attracted an "elite and diverse group of major institutional and private investors from the Gulf, North America, Asia and Europe." The firm described Fund I as built to provide "curated AI exposure through investments in differentiated and restricted opportunities across the AI technology stack" spanning semiconductors, AI infrastructure and AI-enabling platforms.

## A Portfolio Already at the Center of the AI Boom

The fund has not been idle while raising. MGX has invested in 14 companies since inception, and its portfolio reads like a directory of the AI boom's most valuable names.

The firm participated in Anthropic's $65 billion Series H round, adding to a position it had built through the company's earlier Series G. It made a major investment in OpenAI during that company's funding round at a $300 billion valuation, and it is one of the four founding equity backers of Stargate, the $500 billion data-center venture alongside SoftBank, OpenAI and Oracle. MGX has also backed Elon Musk's xAI.

On the infrastructure side, MGX joined a consortium including BlackRock's Global Infrastructure Partners to acquire Aligned Data Centers for about $40 billion, one of the largest digital-infrastructure deals on record. It is a member of the $30 billion AI Infrastructure Partnership building data centers and power, and it is co-developing what could become Europe's largest AI campus near Paris with Nvidia and French partners, a project now envisioned to reach up to 3 gigawatts of compute capacity nationwide. Beyond pure AI, MGX made a $2 billion investment in Binance and took a stake in the TikTok-USDS joint venture that kept the app running in the United States.

## Why It Matters

The MGX close is the clearest signal yet that the Gulf intends to be a principal, not a passenger, in the AI era. For decades, sovereign wealth funds in Abu Dhabi, Saudi Arabia and Qatar recycled oil revenue into passive stakes in Western equities, real estate and trophy assets. MGX represents a sharper thesis: use sovereign capital to buy a strategic position in the technology expected to define the next economy, and do it at a moment when even the richest US companies are straining to finance the compute and energy that frontier AI requires.

That combination of patience and scale is precisely what capital-hungry model developers need. AI labs are burning cash at a rate few private markets can sustain, and infrastructure bills run into the hundreds of billions. A single investor able to write multibillion-dollar checks across chips, data centers and model companies is enormously valuable to founders, and it hands Abu Dhabi leverage over the AI supply chain that money alone rarely buys.

It also concentrates power. When one Gulf firm holds meaningful stakes in OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI and the physical infrastructure they depend on, the line between competitor and common shareholder blurs. That raises real questions in Washington, where MGX's ties to G42 and to Chinese technology have already drawn scrutiny, and where the Aligned and Stargate deals put Emirati capital deep inside critical US infrastructure. The capital is welcome; the concentration and the geopolitics are not frictionless.

## What to Watch

The immediate question is deployment. With $49 billion committed and a $100 billion ambition, MGX will need to keep finding differentiated deals in a market where valuations have already run hot, testing whether it can generate returns or is simply the marginal buyer inflating a bubble. Watch how much of the fund flows into hard infrastructure versus increasingly expensive model equity, whether US regulators tighten conditions on Emirati stakes in strategic assets, and whether Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund and other Gulf players respond with mega-funds of their own. If they do, the center of gravity for AI financing may keep drifting toward the Gulf.
