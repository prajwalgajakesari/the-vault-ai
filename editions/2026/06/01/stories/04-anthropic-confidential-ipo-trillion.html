# Anthropic Files Confidentially for IPO at Nearly One Trillion Dollar Valuation

The maker of Claude submitted a draft S-1 registration to the SEC on Monday, firing the starting gun on what could become the largest AI initial public offering in history and intensifying its race against OpenAI for Wall Street dominance.

---

Anthropic, the artificial intelligence company behind the widely used Claude chatbot, confidentially filed a draft registration statement on Form S-1 with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission on Monday, setting the stage for a public offering that could value the firm at or above one trillion dollars. The filing, disclosed through a terse company blog post, arrives just days after Anthropic closed a staggering \$65 billion Series H funding round that pegged its valuation at \$965 billion -- eclipsing rival OpenAI for the first time.

"This gives us the option to go public after the SEC completes its review," the company said in its announcement. "The number of shares to be offered and the price have not yet been set."

The confidential submission, made under Rule 135 of the Securities Act of 1933, means the full details of Anthropic's finances will remain hidden from the public until the SEC finishes its review and the company decides to proceed. But the signal it sends is unmistakable: the five-year-old startup founded by siblings Dario and Daniela Amodei is positioning itself to beat OpenAI to the public markets, potentially as early as this fall.

A Revenue Rocket

The numbers underscoring Anthropic's trajectory are remarkable even by the inflated standards of the AI industry. The company's annualized revenue run rate surpassed \$47 billion in May, according to multiple reports -- a more than fivefold increase from the \$9 billion it reported at the end of 2025. That explosive growth has been fueled by surging enterprise demand for Claude, which has become a go-to tool for coding, customer support, and complex analytical work across industries.

In a December 2025 Fortune cover story, CEO Dario Amodei made a bold prediction about overtaking his former employer. "I would argue it's maybe even the most likely world in which our revenue passes theirs a year from now," he said of OpenAI. "I think I'd rather have the largest revenue than the largest data center, because one is black [on an income statement], and the other is red."

Six months later, that prediction appears to be materializing. Anthropic's valuation now stands \$113 billion above OpenAI's, which was last valued at \$852 billion after raising \$122 billion in March.

The Race to Ring the Bell

Anthropic's filing did not arrive in a vacuum. It lands in what is shaping up to be the most consequential IPO season in a generation. SpaceX filed its own S-1 in May targeting a \$2 trillion valuation and seeking to raise more than \$75 billion. OpenAI, meanwhile, is reportedly preparing its own confidential filing and also eyeing a fall debut.

Harrison Rolfes, senior late-stage company research analyst at PitchBook, noted that two potentially trillion-dollar filings in such a short window "represents the largest concentration of pre-IPO capital ever brought to market simultaneously."

The jockeying for position matters. As NBC News reported, many analysts believe whichever company reaches the public markets first will perform better, because both Anthropic and OpenAI will be seeking tens of billions of dollars in new capital in quick succession. The parallel to the 2019 Uber-Lyft IPO showdown is instructive: Lyft, which listed first, saw better early performance, while Uber finished its first trading day below its IPO price.

Wedbush Securities analyst Dan Ives framed Anthropic's move as "an opening of the floodgates for the IPO market, which has been relatively dormant for a few years."

Analysis: The Stakes Beyond the Ticker

A successful Anthropic IPO at or above one trillion dollars would represent the market's definitive verdict that the AI boom is producing real, durable businesses -- not just speculative hype. Anthropic's revenue trajectory is among the fastest to scale in the history of technology companies, rivaling even the early growth curves of cloud giants like AWS and Azure.

But the filing also raises uncomfortable questions. Anthropic has burned through enormous amounts of capital building frontier AI models, and the full picture of its profitability remains opaque. Reports from the Wall Street Journal have suggested Anthropic's financials are stronger than OpenAI's, but "stronger" is relative in an industry where training runs alone can cost billions of dollars. Analyst Gil Luria of DA Davidson noted that Anthropic could "set the agenda for how a frontier model reports financials and do so in a way that is favorable to their financial model" -- a reminder that first-mover advantage in IPOs extends to narrative framing as well.

There is also the question of market absorption. With SpaceX, Anthropic, and potentially OpenAI all seeking tens of billions from public investors within months, the sheer volume of capital demanded is unprecedented.

What Comes Next

For now, the confidential S-1 remains exactly that -- confidential. As Fortune's Allie Garfinkle cautioned, "A confidential S-1 is still a maybe. We have no real numbers yet, and timing remains touch-and-go." The filing can be withdrawn at any time, and Anthropic could ultimately go public this summer, this fall, or not at all.

But the chess match is underway. Anthropic has made its opening move, beating OpenAI to the confidential filing stage. The next move belongs to Sam Altman's company, which could accelerate its own timeline to avoid ceding the narrative. Meanwhile, investors -- both institutional and retail -- are left to contemplate a fall IPO season that could reshape the hierarchy of the world"s most valuable companies and test the limits of the market's appetite for artificial intelligence.

One thing is clear: the era of trillion-dollar AI startups going public has arrived, and the way these offerings play out will define the financial landscape of the industry for years to come.
