Nvidia is making its boldest move yet into the personal computer market. The company unveiled the RTX Spark on Sunday, a new PC processor it describes as a "superchip" designed to bring AI agent capabilities directly to consumer desktops and laptops — and it has lined up virtually every major PC manufacturer to build around it.

The RTX Spark combines Nvidia's latest GPU architecture with an Arm-based CPU and dedicated AI acceleration hardware in a single package. ASUS, Dell, HP, Lenovo, Microsoft Surface, and MSI will ship RTX Spark-powered systems this fall, with Acer and Gigabyte models following shortly after.

"We are at the beginning of a new era of computing," said Jensen Huang, Nvidia's CEO, during the announcement. "The PC is being reinvented as an AI computer, and RTX Spark is the engine that will power it."

## A Play for the $200 Billion CPU Market

The move represents Nvidia's direct challenge to Intel and AMD in the traditional PC processor space, a market estimated at $200 billion annually. While Nvidia has dominated discrete graphics cards and data center AI chips for years, the RTX Spark marks its first integrated system-on-chip designed for mainstream personal computers.

The chip is built on Nvidia's Blackwell GPU architecture paired with Arm CPU cores, bypassing the x86 instruction set that has defined PC computing for four decades. Microsoft is providing full Windows support for the platform, including optimizations for its Copilot AI assistant and the new Windows Agent Framework previewed at Build 2026.

"This is not just another chip launch — it is a platform shift," said Patrick Moorhead, founder of Moor Insights and Strategy. "Nvidia is betting that the AI PC category will be large enough to justify taking on two entrenched incumbents simultaneously."

## Specifications and Performance Claims

Nvidia claims the RTX Spark delivers up to 200 trillion operations per second of AI compute while maintaining all-day battery life in laptop form factors. The chip integrates 16 Arm CPU cores, a next-generation GPU with 48 ray tracing cores, and a dedicated neural processing unit capable of running large language models with up to 70 billion parameters entirely on-device.

Early benchmarks shared with select reviewers suggest the RTX Spark outperforms Apple's M4 Ultra in multi-threaded CPU tasks by roughly 15 percent while offering three times the AI inference throughput. Gaming performance reportedly matches Nvidia's own discrete RTX 5070 GPU.

The company is positioning the chip specifically for local AI agent workloads — tasks like autonomous code completion, document analysis, and multi-step workflow automation that currently require cloud connectivity. "The AI PC is not about running a chatbot locally," Huang emphasized. "It is about having an agent that can see your screen, understand your work, and take action on your behalf."

## Why This Matters

The AI PC market is projected to reach $150 billion by 2028, according to IDC, as enterprises seek to move sensitive AI workloads off cloud servers and onto employee devices. Nvidia's entry with a full system-on-chip — rather than just a discrete GPU — signals the company believes the PC platform itself must be redesigned around AI from the ground up.

For Intel and AMD, the timing is particularly challenging. Both companies have released their own AI-enhanced processors, but neither matches Nvidia's AI compute density. Intel's Core Ultra 300 series and AMD's Ryzen AI 9000 chips top out at roughly 50 TOPS of AI performance — a quarter of what Nvidia claims for the RTX Spark.

The partnership with Microsoft is equally significant. Redmond's decision to optimize Windows specifically for the RTX Spark suggests a deeper strategic alignment between the two companies, potentially at the expense of Intel's decades-long Wintel alliance.

## What to Watch Next

PC manufacturers will begin taking pre-orders for RTX Spark systems at Computex in early June, with general availability expected in September. The critical question is pricing: Nvidia has not disclosed chip costs, and whether RTX Spark laptops can compete with existing Arm-based Windows devices below $1,500 will determine whether this remains a premium niche or becomes a genuine platform shift. Intel and AMD's competitive responses, likely at CES Asia later this month, will also shape the trajectory of the AI PC arms race.
