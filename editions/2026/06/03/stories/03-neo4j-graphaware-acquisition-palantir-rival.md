# Neo4j Acquires GraphAware to Challenge Palantir Gotham With AI-Powered Graph Intelligence

*The Vault — AI Edition | June 3, 2026 | 4 min read | 873 words*

**Category:** business

**Key Takeaway:** Neo4j is acquiring GraphAware to build an open-standards alternative to Palantir Gotham for government intelligence analysis, marking a key milestone in the company's 100 million dollar AI investment roadmap.

---

The era of black-box intelligence analysis may be nearing its end. Neo4j, the world's leading graph database company, announced on June 3 that it has agreed to acquire GraphAware, a longtime partner and leading provider of intelligence analysis software for government agencies. The deal, which is expected to close in the third quarter of 2026, will create what the companies describe as a trusted, open-standard alternative to Palantir Gotham -- the proprietary platform that has dominated government intelligence analysis for more than a decade.

The acquisition is a direct shot across Palantir's bow. Where Palantir Gotham has long been criticized by government clients as a proprietary black box that limits data sovereignty and locks agencies into vendor dependency, the combined Neo4j-GraphAware offering promises full transparency, open standards, and sovereign deployment options.

"For over a decade, governments have utilized intelligence analysis software from Palantir. Now they will have a legitimate choice with GraphAware Hume, powered by the Neo4j graph intelligence platform," said Emil Eifrem, Founder and CEO of Neo4j. "We aren't just launching a new intelligence analysis alternative to Palantir Gotham. We're acquiring a proven solution that's built on open-standards technology, and already trusted by government agencies throughout the world."

## A Decade-Long Partnership Becomes a Merger

GraphAware was founded more than ten years ago as the world's first dedicated Neo4j consulting company before pivoting to become a product company. Its flagship platform, GraphAware Hume, is a graph-powered intelligence analysis tool used across law enforcement, national security, cyber defense, and counter-financial crime operations. The platform brings fragmented data together into a connected intelligence picture spanning people, organizations, locations, and events.

The deal represents a key milestone in Neo4j's previously announced 100 million dollar AI investment roadmap. It accelerates the company's push to deliver autonomous, context-aware AI agents capable of turning raw, siloed data into explainable, actionable intelligence.

Michal Bachman, Founder and CEO of GraphAware, framed the acquisition as a natural evolution. "The future of intelligence analysis is undeniably graph-powered and AI-assisted. Having partnered with Neo4j for over ten years, integrating GraphAware Hume into the Neo4j ecosystem is an extremely exciting next step," Bachman said. "By joining forces, we will drive tighter integration and faster innovation, delivering game-changing AI capabilities to our existing joint customers and new organizations worldwide."

## Already Deployed Across Western Governments

The combined platform is not a speculative product. Neo4j and GraphAware Hume are already deployed across multiple high-stakes government operations. The Western Australia Police Force uses the technology to accelerate crime analysis across the world's largest policing jurisdiction, reducing data analysis turnaround from hours to seconds. The U.S. Department of Defense runs Neo4j and GraphAware Hume on a DoD IL5 network inside the U.S. Air Force's Cloud One platform for mission-critical analytics. A European national cyber defense agency relies on the stack to detect and understand threats before they impact critical infrastructure.

Additional customers include the European Commission, the Internal Revenue Service, and the DoD's Platform One. The breadth of these deployments -- spanning law enforcement, defense, tax enforcement, and cyber defense across the U.S., U.K., EU, and Australia -- gives Neo4j a credible foundation for challenging Palantir's dominance.

## Analysis: Why This Matters Now

The timing is deliberate. Growing geopolitical tensions have made data sovereignty a front-burner issue for Western governments. Agencies increasingly demand control over where their software runs, how their data is accessed, and how their systems connect. Several governments have reportedly begun canceling existing contracts with proprietary intelligence vendors and actively seeking alternatives.

Neo4j is positioning itself to capture that demand. The company supports ISO-certified Graph Query Language (GQL) and offers deployment flexibility across AWS GovCloud, private clouds, and on-premises environments -- a critical requirement for agencies operating under strict data residency rules. The company reports a 4x increase in customers solving for AI-ready data use cases, with a significant portion already in production.

The competitive landscape is also shifting. Neo4j announced its 100 million dollar AI investment in October 2025, which has already produced Neo4j Aura Agent, an MCP Server for Neo4j, and an AI start-up program with over 700 registered companies. Palantir, meanwhile, has seen its stock soar on the strength of its AIP platform for commercial clients, but its government business faces growing scrutiny over vendor lock-in concerns.

## What Happens Next

Post-acquisition, GraphAware will operate as a standalone business unit within Neo4j, led by Bachman. The companies say business operations will remain largely unchanged in the near term, with the merger being additive for customers who will gain access to more resources and a richer ecosystem. The deal is subject to customary closing conditions and regulatory approvals, including foreign direct investment approval in Australia.

The longer-term vision is ambitious: an open, AI-powered intelligence analysis platform with deeper capabilities, tighter integrations, and the flexibility for agencies to deploy on their own terms. Whether Neo4j can truly dethrone Palantir in the government intelligence market remains to be seen, but with production deployments already in hand and a 100 million dollar war chest behind it, this is no longer a theoretical challenge.
