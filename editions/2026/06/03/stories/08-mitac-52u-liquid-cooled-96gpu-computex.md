# MiTAC Debuts 52U Liquid-Cooled AI Rack With 96 AMD GPUs at Computex 2026

**Taiwan-based server maker packs 12 AI servers and 96 AMD Instinct MI355X GPUs into a single 52U liquid-cooled rack, claiming 50% higher GPU density than standard configurations, while showcasing diamond-cooled servers that generate up to 50% more tokens than stock hardware.**

TAIPEI -- At Computex 2026, MiTAC Computing Technology Corp. is making a bold statement about the future of AI infrastructure: density is destiny. The subsidiary of MiTAC Holdings Corporation unveiled its flagship 52U High-Density AI Liquid-Cooled Rack, a towering assembly that crams 12 MiTAC G4826Z5 AI servers loaded with 96 AMD Instinct MI355X GPUs into a single rack, delivering what the company says is a 50% improvement in GPU density over standard AI server configurations.

The announcement, made at the company\u2019s booth in Hall 2 of the Taipei Nangang Exhibition Center, positions MiTAC squarely at the intersection of two forces reshaping the data center industry: the relentless demand for AI compute and the physical constraints of power, cooling, and floor space that threaten to throttle it.

"At COMPUTEX 2026, MiTAC is presenting a diversified AI infrastructure engineered to drive dynamic AI workloads through strategic ecosystem partnerships," said Rick Hwang, President of MiTAC Computing Technology. "By integrating high-performance AI platforms with a robust hardware-software ecosystem, we deliver flexible, out-of-the-box turnkey infrastructure tailored for modern data centers."

## The Density Problem

The timing is not accidental. As enterprises race from generative AI experimentation toward autonomous agentic AI deployments, the demand for massive computing power, ultra-low latency, and high-throughput inference has surged. But traditional data center scaling is hitting hard physical limits. Adding more racks means more floor space, more cooling capacity, and more strain on already-stretched power grids.

MiTAC\u2019s answer is vertical integration in the most literal sense. The 52U rack -- taller than the standard 42U form factor common in most data centers -- uses liquid cooling to manage the enormous thermal output of 96 GPUs operating simultaneously. Every component, from compute nodes and network switches to coolant distribution units and power management systems, is pre-integrated and calibrated as a single turnkey unit. The goal is to let data center operators deploy an entire GPU cluster without the weeks of custom engineering that rack-scale AI deployments typically require.

## Diamond Cooling: From Space to the Server Room

Perhaps the more eye-catching technology on display is MiTAC\u2019s G8825Z5 Diamond-cooled AI server, developed in partnership with Akash Systems and powered by AMD Instinct MI350X GPUs. The system uses Akash\u2019s proprietary Diamond Cooling technology, which applies synthetic diamond -- the most thermally conductive material known -- to GPU heat management. Diamond removes heat five times faster than copper, the current industry standard.

The results, according to the companies, are significant. Diamond-cooled servers generate up to 50% more tokens than stock hardware and deliver throttle-free performance even at inlet air temperatures exceeding 95 degrees Fahrenheit. Internal testing showed a temperature reduction of up to 10 degrees Celsius (18 degrees Fahrenheit) on both GPU and High Bandwidth Memory, translating to up to 22% additional FLOPs per watt at standard data center temperatures.

"AI demand is rapidly outpacing the infrastructure that powers it," said Dr. Felix Ejeckam, Co-Founder and CEO of Akash Systems. "Akash\u2019s patented solution breaks through the thermal bottleneck to accelerate AI deployments, profitably. Together with AMD and MiTAC, we are bringing world-class technology and expertise to build the foundation AI needs to scale."

The diamond cooling technology was originally developed for satellite systems in partnership with NASA before being adapted for terrestrial data centers. Akash received a USD 300 million initial launch order for its diamond-cooled solutions when the MiTAC partnership was first announced in March 2026. Each Diamond-Cooled server is estimated to generate up to USD 1 million in incremental value over four years compared to conventionally cooled alternatives.

## Ecosystem and Software Stack

Beyond raw hardware, MiTAC used Computex to showcase its broader infrastructure ecosystem. The company\u2019s open-source baseboard management firmware, MiOBMC, and its proprietary MiCoreView POD management platform provide operators with bare-metal control over GPU clusters without vendor lock-in. MiCoreView integrates with Kubernetes and the AMD Enterprise AI Suite for automated GPU resource orchestration.

Strategic partnerships with DDN for storage, Rafay for GPU-as-a-Service orchestration, and Canonical for Ubuntu integration round out the turnkey offering. MiTAC also announced a collaboration with Tonomia on the TonoForge Modular Data Center, which pairs MiTAC\u2019s liquid-cooled server racks with Tonomia\u2019s energy management system to cut deployment timelines to 12 weeks.

## What It Means

MiTAC\u2019s Computex showing reflects a broader industry trend: AI infrastructure vendors are competing not just on chip performance but on the full-stack integration required to actually deploy these systems at scale. With power and cooling emerging as the binding constraints on AI expansion, companies that can deliver more compute per watt and per square foot hold a structural advantage.

The 52U rack\u2019s 50% density improvement is a meaningful step, though independent benchmarks will be needed to validate performance claims under production workloads. The diamond cooling technology, if it scales commercially, could represent a genuine inflection point in thermal management -- a domain where incremental gains have been the norm for years.

For now, MiTAC is betting that the future of AI infrastructure belongs to companies that can deliver entire data center building blocks, not just servers. At Computex 2026, that bet is taking physical shape.
