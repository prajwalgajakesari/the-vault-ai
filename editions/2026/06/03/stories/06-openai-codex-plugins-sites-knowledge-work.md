# OpenAI Expands Codex Beyond Code With Six Knowledge-Work Plugins and Sites Preview

OpenAI is no longer content to let Codex be a developer-only tool. On Tuesday, the company rolled out six role-specific plugins, a new Sites feature for building interactive hosted workspaces, and an expanded Annotations capability -- collectively representing the most aggressive enterprise pivot yet for the platform that began life as an agentic coding assistant.

The numbers underscore why. More than 5 million people now use Codex every week, a figure that has grown more than sixfold since the desktop app launched in February. And the fastest-growing segment is not engineers: non-developers -- analysts, marketers, designers, investors, and bankers -- now account for roughly 20 percent of all Codex users and are expanding at more than three times the rate of their developer counterparts.

"AI is becoming capable of doing increasingly meaningful work inside organizations," OpenAI Chief Revenue Officer Denise Dresser said in a statement. "The challenge now is helping companies integrate these systems into the infrastructure and workflows that power their businesses."

## Six Plugins, 62 Apps, 110 Skills

The six new plugins cover data analytics, creative production, sales, product design, public equity investing, and investment banking. Together they bundle integrations with 62 popular enterprise applications -- including Snowflake, Databricks, Figma, Salesforce, HubSpot, Canva, Tableau, and FactSet -- and package 110 automated skills that work out of the box.

Each plugin is designed for a specific job function. The data analytics plugin lets business teams query product and performance data, explain metric changes, and build dashboards using tools like Snowflake and Hex. The sales plugin pulls context from Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, and Outreach to help reps prepare for meetings, build close plans, and review at-risk deals. The creative production plugin connects to Figma, Canva, Shutterstock, and Picsart so marketing teams can turn a brief into reviewable ad variations and lifestyle shots. The investment banking and equity investing plugins draw on data from Moody"s, Daloopa, FactSet, LSEG, S&P, PitchBook, and Hebbia to support pitch materials, comparable analysis, and thesis tracking.

More plugins are already on the roadmap -- corporate finance, private equity, marketing strategy, strategy consulting, and legal -- and OpenAI says it is building toward an open ecosystem where partners can create and deploy their own plugins directly in Codex and ChatGPT.

## Sites: A New Canvas for Enterprise Work

Perhaps the most ambitious addition is Sites, now available in preview for Business and Enterprise customers. Sites lets Codex generate interactive, hosted websites and applications that can be shared across a workspace via URL -- think custom dashboards, scenario planners, product launch trackers, or customer service guides.

"Instead of adapting work to the limits of a single tool or file, teams can create sites that fit the work," OpenAI wrote in its announcement.

To strengthen the feature, OpenAI is partnering with Vercel, Wix, Base44, Replit, Lovable, Figma, Webflow, and Emergent as part of a nascent sites partner ecosystem. The vision is clear: Codex becomes not just a chatbot or a code generator, but a full workspace where teams create, refine, and publish interactive artifacts without ever touching a traditional development environment.

## Annotations Expand to Knowledge Work

The third pillar of the update extends the Annotations feature -- previously available only for code and Markdown files -- to documents, spreadsheets, and slides. Users can now highlight a specific section of any supported file type and direct Codex to make targeted changes without disturbing the surrounding content.

The practical implications are significant. An analyst can select a block of cells in a financial model and ask Codex to generate a chart of revenue and EBITDA over the selected years; the model executes strictly within that boundary, leaving adjacent formulas and formatting untouched. It is a refinement-layer capability that makes Codex more useful after the first draft, when work requires judgment and iteration rather than generation from scratch.

## Competitive Context

The timing is notable. Anthropic launched its own Enterprise Agents program with knowledge-work plugins for Claude back in February, followed by finance-specific agents in May. As TechCrunch"s Russell Brandom observed, OpenAI "has been slower to court enterprise customers, only introducing plugin support for Codex in March." The parallel between the two companies" vertical plugin lists -- both targeting finance, design, and marketing -- reflects a shared bet that document-centric professional workflows are where AI agents can currently deliver the most tangible value.

The update also arrives just three weeks after OpenAI announced the OpenAI Deployment Company, a joint venture backed by more than four billion dollars in funding from global investment firms, aimed at embedding OpenAI tools more deeply into enterprise operations worldwide.

## What It Means

OpenAI"s Codex expansion marks a decisive shift from developer tool to enterprise platform. By bundling role-specific integrations, hosting interactive workspaces, and enabling precision editing across non-code documents, the company is making a clear argument: the future of workplace AI is not a chatbot that answers questions, but an agent that produces, refines, and publishes work product end to end. With 5 million weekly users and a non-developer base growing three times faster than its engineering cohort, the market is already voting with its usage. The question now is whether OpenAI can build the partner ecosystem and enterprise trust fast enough to outpace Anthropic and Google in the race for the knowledge worker"s desktop.
