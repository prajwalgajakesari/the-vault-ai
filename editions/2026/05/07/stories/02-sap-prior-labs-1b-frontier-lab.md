# SAP Acquires Prior Labs With EUR1 Billion Commitment to Build Europe Leading Frontier AI Lab

*The Vault -- AI Edition | May 7, 2026 | 4 min read | 823 words*

**Category:** business

**Key Takeaway:** SAP is betting more than EUR1 billion on Prior Labs to build a European frontier AI lab focused on tabular foundation models, a category the German software giant believes will prove more valuable for enterprise than large language models.

---

German enterprise software titan SAP is making its boldest AI move yet, acquiring 18-month-old startup Prior Labs and committing more than EUR1 billion over four years to transform it into what the company calls a globally leading frontier AI lab for structured data -- right in the heart of Europe.

The deal, announced on May 4, brings one of the most promising AI research teams in the tabular foundation model (TFM) space under the SAP umbrella. While the acquisition price was not publicly disclosed, sources told TechCrunch the transaction was an almost all-cash deal with well over half a billion dollars paid up front to the three co-founders: Frank Hutter, Noah Hollmann, and Sauraj Gambhir.

Balderton Capital partner James Wise called the exit one of Germany s biggest ever venture outcomes -- a remarkable result for a startup that raised just USD9.3 million in a pre-seed round led by Balderton in February 2025.

## The Structured Data Thesis

SAP is making a contrarian bet in an industry laser-focused on large language models. The company argues that while LLMs dominate headlines, they struggle with the tables, numbers, and statistics that form the backbone of enterprise operations. Tabular foundation models, by contrast, are purpose-built for this structured data and can predict business outcomes like payment delays, supplier risks, customer churn, and upsell opportunities.

SAP CTO Philipp Herzig framed the strategic logic in stark terms: "Early on, SAP recognized that the greatest untapped opportunity in enterprise AI was not large language models; it was AI built for the structured data that runs the world s businesses. We built SAP-RPT-1 to prove that conviction for enterprise data. Prior Labs has built a leading TFM on public benchmarks and built one of the leading research teams in this category. Combining their frontier model work with enterprise data and customer reach is how we intend to lead this category globally."

Prior Labs CEO Frank Hutter echoed the ambition: "Joining the SAP family gives us the resources, data environment and customer reach to take this category to its full potential."

## What Prior Labs Brings to the Table

Prior Labs, headquartered in Freiburg, Germany with offices in Berlin and New York, has assembled a formidable research team recruited from Google, Apple, Amazon, Microsoft, G-Research, Jane Street, Goldman Sachs, and CERN. Its flagship TabPFN model series, published in Nature, has set state-of-the-art performance on tabular benchmarks across hundreds of independent academic studies and has been downloaded over three million times as an open-source tool.

The latest version, TabPFN-2.6, ranks first on TabArena, the leading benchmark for TFMs. SAP says the model matches the accuracy of a four-hour automated machine learning pipeline instantly, in a single model, at a fraction of the complexity.

The scientific firepower extends to the advisory level. Yann LeCun, the ACM Turing Award winner and executive chairman at Advanced Machine Intelligence, and Bernhard Schoelkopf, director of the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems and ELLIS president, will both serve on Prior Labs scientific advisory board as the lab scales.

SAP confirmed that Prior Labs open-source strategy around TabPFN will continue, a signal designed to maintain the developer ecosystem that has made the model widely adopted.

## Analysis: Playing Offense and Defense

The acquisition reflects both offensive ambition and defensive necessity. SAP is not a newcomer to AI investments -- it backed Anthropic in 2023, along with Aleph Alpha and Cohere -- but this deal represents a shift from portfolio investing to building proprietary capability in-house.

The timing is notable. SAP stock has dropped in 2026 amid what some analysts have called the SaaSpocalypse, raising questions about traditional enterprise software companies ability to adapt. The Prior Labs deal, combined with SAP s simultaneous acquisition of data platform Dremio, signals that the company is restructuring around AI and data infrastructure rather than waiting for disruption to arrive.

SAP is also locking down its AI ecosystem. The company now prohibits unauthorized AI agents from accessing its products through its API, allowing only SAP-endorsed architectures like its own Joule Agents and Nvidia NemoClaw. This walled-garden approach stands in sharp contrast to Salesforce, which is letting customers bring any agent they choose.

The EUR1 billion commitment over four years is substantial but not without precedent in an era of multibillion-dollar AI investments. The real test will be whether Prior Labs can translate academic benchmarks into production-grade enterprise AI while maintaining the research velocity that made it attractive in the first place.

The transaction is expected to close in Q2 or Q3 of 2026, pending regulatory approvals.
