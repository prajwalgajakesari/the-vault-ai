The World Intelligence Expo 2026 concluded its four-day run in Tianjin on May 31, sending a clear message to the global technology community: China's AI ecosystem is expanding at a pace that demands attention. The event drew more than 700 exhibitors and featured a striking statistic that organizers repeated throughout -- China now holds approximately 60 percent of all AI patents worldwide.

Jointly hosted by Tianjin and Chongqing, the expo featured one comprehensive exhibition zone and six thematic areas covering core AI technology, embodied intelligence, intelligent connected vehicles, low-altitude economy, smart living, and smart manufacturing. The scale of the event reflected the Chinese government's determination to position AI as a cornerstone of economic growth.

"The scale of China's core AI industry has exceeded 1.2 trillion yuan," noted Vice Minister of Science and Technology Wu Zhaohui in his opening address, citing a figure equivalent to roughly $176 billion. "We are seeing AI move from laboratory demonstrations to real-world deployment across every sector of the economy."

## Embodied Intelligence Takes Center Stage

The expo's most notable addition was a dedicated embodied intelligence pavilion -- the first time the technology received its own independent exhibition zone. More than 80 domestic and international enterprises showcased nearly 150 complete robots and related products, ranging from industrial manipulators to consumer-facing service robots.

On the show floor, a smart mirror provided instant health assessments by analyzing a visitor's face, a robotic arm demonstrated the precise preparation of traditional Chinese crepes, and humanoid robots played Go and told stories to children. These demonstrations, while partly theatrical, reflected genuine advances in multimodal perception and dexterous manipulation.

"China has accelerated its embodied AI development through a national framework for dataset cooperation," said Zhang Wei, director of the China Institute of AI and Robotics. "Shared datasets for manipulation, locomotion, and scene understanding are enabling smaller companies to build capable systems without the massive data collection budgets of larger labs."

## Patent Dominance in Context

China's 60 percent share of global AI patents represents a dramatic shift from a decade ago, when the United States and Europe held commanding leads in AI intellectual property. The growth has been driven by a combination of aggressive government funding, a large pool of engineering talent, and corporate patent strategies at companies like Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent, and Huawei.

However, patent volume alone does not capture the full picture. Quality metrics, including citation rates and commercialization outcomes, tell a more nuanced story. Many of China's AI patents cover incremental improvements rather than foundational breakthroughs, and translation from patent to product remains uneven across the ecosystem.

Still, the sheer scale of activity is significant. Chinese researchers published more AI papers than any other country in 2025, and Chinese companies now dominate several application categories, including facial recognition, natural language processing in Mandarin, and autonomous driving.

## Geopolitical Implications

The expo took place against a backdrop of escalating technology tensions between China and the United States. Export controls on advanced chips have pushed Chinese AI companies to develop alternative hardware, while US restrictions on AI talent mobility have complicated cross-border collaboration.

China's response has been to double down on domestic capability building, with the Tianjin expo serving as both a showcase and a recruiting tool for the country's AI ambitions.

## What to Watch

The expo's emphasis on embodied intelligence and manufacturing AI signals where China sees its competitive advantage -- in physical-world applications rather than pure language model development. As Chinese AI companies increasingly compete on the global stage, the gap between patent volume and practical deployment will be the metric that matters most.
