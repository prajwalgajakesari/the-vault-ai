# Wirestock Raises $23 Million Series A to Scale Multimodal AI Data Platform

The creator-powered data startup has quietly become a critical supplier to six of the world's largest foundation model makers, riding a 20x surge in creator payouts and a $40 million annual run rate as AI labs scramble for licensed, high-quality multimodal training data.

---

Wirestock, the Los Angeles-based platform that connects a global network of human creators with AI laboratories hungry for licensed training data, has closed a $23 million Series A funding round led by Nava Ventures. The round, which brings the company's total capital raised to approximately $26 million, drew participation from SBVP, the venture firm co-founded by former Meta COO Sheryl Sandberg, along with Formula VC, I2BF Global Ventures, and other strategic and existing investors.

The funding arrives at a moment when the AI industry's appetite for ethically sourced, high-quality multimodal data has become nearly insatiable. As frontier models push beyond text into images, video, 3D assets, and audio, the companies building them face a dual challenge: finding enough diverse creative data to train on and doing so without triggering the copyright lawsuits that have plagued the sector. Wirestock has positioned itself squarely at that intersection, and the market has noticed.

From Stock Photography to AI Infrastructure

Wirestock's origin story is a pivot. The company began life as a distributor for stock photography, helping creators sell their work across multiple marketplaces. But in 2023, as the AI training data gold rush accelerated, the company reinvented itself as a data platform, channeling the creative output of its community directly into the pipelines of foundation model makers.

The transformation has been dramatic. Wirestock now operates a community of more than 700,000 creators spanning photographers, videographers, graphic designers, 3D artists, filmmakers, and musicians who have collectively contributed millions of assets to the platform. The company currently supplies multimodal datasets to six of the largest foundation model makers in the world, though confidentiality agreements prevent it from naming them publicly.

"Human creativity and technological progress reinforce rather than undermine each other," said Mikayel Khachatryan, Wirestock's CEO and co-founder. "We remain deeply committed to fair compensation, transparency, and providing ethical data that drives responsible AI development."

The numbers back the narrative. Shifting to an AI licensing model has driven a 20x year-over-year increase in creator payouts, with millions of dollars now flowing to contributors monthly. The company has paid out $15 million to its creators to date and has pushed its annual run rate above $40 million, a figure that underscores the scale of demand from AI labs willing to pay a premium for licensed, curated datasets.

Custom Data as the Growth Engine

What started as an off-the-shelf data marketplace has evolved into something more bespoke. Khachatryan has noted that the business shifted from selling pre-existing datasets to fulfilling increasingly complex custom requests from AI lab partners. That shift toward tailored, semantically rich datasets represents a stickier and higher-margin business model, one that transforms Wirestock from a commodity supplier into something closer to a research partner.

The $23 million in fresh capital will expand the company's capacity to execute on those custom engagements, extend coverage into emerging creative modalities, and deepen the integrations that make Wirestock's data pipelines a seamless extension of its AI lab partners' research workflows. The 60-person company also plans to hire across research, engineering, and product roles, and is building enterprise software that lets AI labs collaborate directly on dataset creation and curation.

Investor Conviction: Multimodal Data as Strategic Moat

For lead investor Nava Ventures, the thesis centers on a structural shift in what AI models will need to advance.

"As artificial intelligence progresses past language-based systems, multimodal data will become an increasingly important part of any model's training," said Freddie Martignetti of Nava Ventures. "Wirestock has a deep understanding of what foundational models and hyperscalers need in terms of multimodal data to start creating more human-like systems. The cornerstone of our thesis was that multimodal data will be increasingly important, not just to create images or videos, but for models to complete real-world tasks."

The participation of Sheryl Sandberg's SBVP adds another signal. The firm's involvement suggests that seasoned operators see Wirestock not merely as an AI infrastructure play but as a platform with network effects: the more creators it attracts, the richer and more diverse its datasets become, which in turn draws more AI lab customers, which funds higher creator payouts, completing a flywheel that competitors would struggle to replicate from scratch.

The Broader Data Supply Chain

Wirestock's raise lands in a market that has grown intensely competitive. Scale AI, which was valued at $13.8 billion in 2024, remains the dominant force in AI data labeling and curation. Defined.ai, Appen, and a crop of newer startups are also vying for pieces of the training data supply chain. Getty Images and Shutterstock have both launched their own licensed data programs aimed at AI customers.

But Wirestock occupies a somewhat differentiated niche. Rather than relying on crowdsourced micro-task labor for annotation, it leans on a community of skilled creators who produce original, high-quality creative assets across multiple modalities. That distinction matters as AI models grow more sophisticated and the quality bar for training data rises correspondingly.

The risk, of course, is concentration. With six unnamed AI lab customers representing the core of the business, Wirestock's fortunes are tied to the spending patterns and strategic priorities of a small number of deep-pocketed buyers. Any pullback in AI training budgets or a shift toward synthetic data generation could pressure the model.

What Comes Next

For now, the tailwinds are strong. AI labs are spending aggressively on training data, copyright litigation is pushing the industry toward licensed sources, and multimodal capabilities are the next frontier for model differentiation. Wirestock, with its 700,000-creator network, $40 million run rate, and fresh capital, is betting it can ride all three trends simultaneously. Whether a $26 million total raise is enough to build durable infrastructure in a market where competitors measure their war chests in billions remains the open question, but the early returns suggest the company has found a seam in the market that larger players have been slow to exploit.
