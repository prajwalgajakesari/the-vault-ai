# HubSpot Launches Free AEO Sensor Dashboard to Track Brand Visibility Across AI Search Engines

The question used to be whether your brand appeared on the first page of Google. Now the question is whether ChatGPT even knows you exist. On May 14, HubSpot launched AEO Sensor, a free, publicly accessible dashboard that tracks how answer engines behave toward brands in real time, arriving at a moment when the data it monitors has never looked more volatile.

AEO Sensor monitors three dimensions across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity: daily answer engine volatility, weekly AI-referred traffic trends, and weekly visibility and citation data broken down by industry. The dashboard ships with roughly three months of historical data stretching back to February 16, 2026, and requires no login or subscription to access. It is designed as an industry-level instrument, not a brand-specific one, meaning any marketer can consult it without handing over credentials or a credit card.

The launch is not happening in a vacuum. According to StatCounter, ChatGPT's share of AI chatbot referrals to websites worldwide fell to 76.85 percent in April 2026, the lowest level on record and a drop of 1.3 percentage points month-over-month. A year earlier, that figure stood at 84.21 percent. The referral market is fragmenting rapidly: Google Gemini now holds 9 percent, Perplexity has recovered to 7.73 percent, Microsoft Copilot sits at 3.76 percent, and Claude accounts for 2.66 percent. ChatGPT's share has now declined for three consecutive months, shedding nearly eight percentage points over the course of a single year.

For businesses that built their AI visibility strategies around a single platform, the erosion is a warning. HubSpot's own data reinforces the urgency: the company found that organic traffic dropped 27 percent for its customers as AI search consumed traditional web queries. The old playbook of optimizing for one search engine is breaking down, and a new discipline, answer engine optimization, is stepping into the gap.

"How buyers search is fundamentally changing," said Yamini Rangan, CEO of HubSpot. "They are asking questions in places like ChatGPT and Gemini, and the companies that show up in those answers are already winning. That is exactly why we have built HubSpot AEO: to help businesses improve their AI visibility, grow brand awareness, and ultimately drive more qualified leads."

AEO Sensor is positioned as the free, public complement to HubSpot AEO, the paid monitoring and optimization product the company launched in April 2026 at its Spring Spotlight event. The paid tool, available at fifty dollars per month with no broader HubSpot plan required, offers brand-specific tracking: prompt-level visibility data showing the exact responses ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity return when users ask about a company, a visibility score measuring how often a brand appears in AI answers and whether mentions skew positive, negative, or neutral, and actionable content recommendations piped directly into Marketing Hub workflows. Early results from the beta suggest the investment pays off. HubSpot reported that beta users prioritizing answer engines saw AI referral traffic grow 20 percent compared to customers not using the tool.

The strategic logic extends beyond a single product launch. HubSpot acquired XFunnel to bolster its AEO capabilities, and the company has framed answer engine optimization as central to its 2026 roadmap. Kipp Bodnar, HubSpot's chief marketing officer, has been blunt about the stakes: "2026 could represent the biggest single wave of change our industry has ever seen."

The broader market context makes the timing shrewd. ChatGPT's mobile daily active user share in the United States fell below 40 percent for the first time in March 2026, marking its fourth consecutive month of decline. Meanwhile, research firm data shows that 42 percent of CRM software buyers now use AI search as part of their evaluation process. The audience is fragmenting, but it is not shrinking. Buyers are simply spreading across more platforms, and the brands that track all of them will hold an advantage over those still fixated on a single channel.

Why This Matters

AEO Sensor represents a bet that the market for AI search intelligence is large enough to justify giving the top layer away for free. The playbook is familiar from HubSpot's history: offer a free tool that demonstrates the problem, then sell the solution that fixes it. The AEO Grader, another free instrument, already lets individual brands check how they appear in AI answers. Sensor adds the macro view, the weather report for an industry that desperately needs one.

The deeper significance is structural. For two decades, SEO tooling companies like Moz, Ahrefs, and Semrush built empires on the premise that Google's algorithm was the gravitational center of digital marketing. Answer engine optimization fractures that center into multiple competing platforms, each with its own citation logic, its own ranking signals, and its own volatility patterns. HubSpot is positioning itself as the monitoring layer that sits above all of them.

What to Watch

The critical question is whether AEO Sensor's data becomes an industry reference point or fades into the crowded landscape of marketing dashboards. HubSpot's advantage is distribution: more than 248,000 customers across 135 countries already use its platform. If even a fraction of those customers adopt AEO Sensor as their default volatility check, the dashboard becomes a self-reinforcing standard. Watch for whether competitors like Semrush and Ahrefs respond with their own AI visibility dashboards, and whether the 20 percent traffic lift HubSpot claims for its paid AEO beta users holds up as the tool scales beyond early adopters. The race to own the AI search intelligence layer has officially begun.
