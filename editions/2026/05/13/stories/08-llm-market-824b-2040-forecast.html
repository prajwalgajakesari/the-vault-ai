Global LLM Market Forecast to Reach $824 Billion by 2040 at 35 Percent Annual Growth

4 min read 950 words 5 sources
Key Takeaway

The global LLM market is projected to grow from $11.63 billion to $823.93 billion by 2040 at a 35.57% CAGR, driven by collapsing inference costs and agentic AI adoption.

The large language model industry is on pace to grow more than 70-fold in 14 years, powered by collapsing costs, multimodal capabilities, and agentic AI systems that are pulling enterprise adoption forward far faster than analysts expected even 18 months ago.

A comprehensive new forecast published Tuesday by Research and Markets projects the global LLM market will expand from $11.63 billion in 2026 to $823.93 billion by 2040, representing a compound annual growth rate of 35.57 percent. The report, distributed via Roots Analysis and covering 22 major vendors across six continents, arrives at a moment when the technology is no longer a laboratory curiosity but a core infrastructure line item for Fortune 500 budgets.

The Cost Collapse That Changed Everything

Perhaps the most striking catalyst behind the forecast is the dramatic deflation in inference costs. When OpenAI launched GPT-4 in March 2023, developers paid $30 per million input tokens. Equivalent capability through GPT-4o now costs $2.50 per million tokens, a 12-fold reduction in barely three years. Competing providers have driven prices even lower, with some open-weight models running inference for under $1 per million tokens on optimized cloud infrastructure.

That cost collapse has unlocked use cases that were economically unthinkable at launch-era pricing. Companies that once restricted LLM calls to a handful of high-value workflows are now embedding language models into everything from routine customer support triage to real-time supply chain analytics.

"The democratization of AI via cloud-based and API-driven platforms allows startups and small businesses to access language models at low cost, reducing infrastructure entry barriers," the Research and Markets report noted, pointing to the role of cloud deployment in accelerating adoption across sectors.

Multimodal and Agentic Systems Lead the Next Wave

The report identifies two converging trends that will sustain the market's trajectory through the 2030s: multimodal models and agentic AI.

Multimodal LLMs, which process text, images, audio, and video within a single architecture, already dominate new deployments. The technology has moved well beyond research demos. Google's Gemini, Anthropic's Claude, and OpenAI's GPT series all ship production multimodal capabilities, and the Roots Analysis data shows multimodal models commanding the largest share of new enterprise contracts.

Agentic AI, systems capable of autonomously planning, executing, and iterating on multi-step tasks, represents the next frontier. According to industry data, 31 percent of enterprises now have at least one AI agent running in production, with banking and insurance leading adoption at 47 percent. Yet the gap between experimentation and deployment remains vast: roughly 79 percent of enterprises have adopted AI agents in some form, but only about one in nine has pushed them into live production workflows.

"The expanded collaboration between Snowflake and Anthropic, a $200 million strategic partnership, aims to deploy AI agents and provide access to Anthropic's Claude model to more than 12,600 global customers," the report highlighted as a notable example of how major platforms are racing to operationalize agentic capabilities.

Industry Adoption: IT Leads, Healthcare Accelerates

The forecast breaks adoption down by vertical, and the picture is one of broad but uneven penetration. IT and telecommunications holds the largest share at nearly 30 percent of the market, driven by the integration of LLMs into customer service automation, real-time communications, and IT operations platforms.

Retail and e-commerce follows with a 27.5 percent share, leveraging language models for demand forecasting, personalized recommendations, and automated content generation at scale.

But the fastest growth is expected in healthcare, where LLMs are being deployed for clinical coding, biomedical research, patient question-answering, and diagnostic support. In the United States, 21 percent of healthcare organizations already use LLMs to answer patient questions, while 18 percent apply the technology to biomedical research. The sector's stringent regulatory requirements around HIPAA compliance have slowed production deployment, with only 3 percent of healthcare organizations running AI agents in live workflows, but the pipeline of pilots is enormous.

The banking, financial services, and insurance sector converts pilots to production at the highest rate of any industry at 58 percent, underscoring the sector's appetite for automation in fraud detection, compliance monitoring, and customer interactions.

North America Dominates, Asia-Pacific Surges

Geographically, North America commands 33 percent of global LLM revenue, benefiting from concentrated venture capital, established cloud infrastructure, and the headquarters presence of OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Anthropic, and Meta. The region's favorable policy environment and aggressive enterprise AI spending reinforce its lead.

The Asia-Pacific region, however, is forecast to grow at the fastest clip. Massive government-backed AI investments in China, Japan, and South Korea, combined with domestic champions like Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent, and DeepSeek, are fueling a deployment wave that the report expects will narrow the gap with North America significantly before 2040.

The $824 Billion Question

A 70-fold expansion over 14 years is an extraordinary claim, and skeptics will note that the forecast depends on sustained double-digit growth every single year through 2040. History offers few technology markets that have maintained a 35 percent CAGR for that duration without significant interruption.

But the structural tailwinds are formidable. Global generative AI spending is already projected to hit $644 billion in 2025 alone, a 76 percent increase over 2024 according to Gartner. Nearly 60 percent of businesses plan to increase their AI investments next year. And the technology itself continues to improve at a pace that creates new addressable markets faster than existing ones saturate.

The competitive landscape is also intensifying. The report profiles 22 leading vendors including OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, Meta, Microsoft, Mistral AI, DeepSeek, and NVIDIA, and notes that strategic partnerships, open-source model releases, and vertical-specific fine-tuning are all accelerating the pace at which LLM capabilities reach production environments.

Whether the market ultimately lands at $824 billion or some fraction of that figure, the directional signal is clear: large language models have crossed the threshold from experimental technology to economic infrastructure, and the investment cycle is still accelerating.
