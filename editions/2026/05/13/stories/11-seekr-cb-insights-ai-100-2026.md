# Seekr Named to 2026 CB Insights AI 100 for Explainable Enterprise AI Platform

In an AI landscape increasingly defined by questions of trust, auditability, and regulatory compliance, Seekr has earned a spot on the 2026 CB Insights AI 100 -- the annual ranking of the 100 most promising private artificial intelligence companies in the world. The recognition places Seekr squarely in a cohort of startups that CB Insights says have demonstrated real traction beyond the demo stage, and it underscores a broader market shift toward AI systems that can explain their own reasoning.

The selection marks the tenth edition of the AI 100 list, which this year spans companies working on everything from autonomous security operations and humanoid robotics to domain-specific AI for healthcare and financial services. But Seekr occupies a distinctive niche: explainable, defensible AI built specifically for enterprises and government agencies operating in regulated and mission-critical environments.

## A Mosaic Score in the Top One Percent

CB Insights evaluates companies using its proprietary Mosaic scoring system, which assesses growth momentum, financial strength, industry health, and management quality. Seekr landed in the top one percent of all private AI companies assessed -- a signal that the firm is not merely building interesting technology but scaling it commercially.

The 2026 AI 100 cohort collectively has raised \$10.9 billion in equity funding over time, with more than \$2 billion raised in 2026 alone as of late April. A fifth of the companies on the list hail from outside the United States, spanning nine countries across four continents, and the group has forged more than 190 business relationships since 2024 with industry heavyweights including Google, Nvidia, and Databricks.

"The 2026 AI 100 identifies emerging, high-momentum startups worth watching in an increasingly crowded market," said Adya Pandey, AI Analyst at CB Insights. "This year"s cohort spans autonomous security operations, humanoid robots, and domain-specific AI for healthcare and financial services, and what unites them is proof of real traction outside a demo environment."

## SeekrFlow: A Full-Stack AI Operating System

At the core of Seekr"s offering is SeekrFlow, which the company describes as a full-stack AI operating system. The platform enables organizations to build, validate, govern, and deploy domain-specific large language models, vision language models, and AI agents trained on their own data -- across cloud, on-premises, edge, and sovereign deployment environments.

What differentiates SeekrFlow from the growing crop of enterprise AI platforms is its architectural commitment to trust. The platform embeds orchestration to control system behavior, observability to track what the system did, explainability to understand why it made a given decision, contestability to act on discrepancies, and evaluations to verify outcomes. Seekr"s proprietary data preparation tools claim to enhance model accuracy by three times and relevance by six times compared to baseline approaches.

"Enterprise and government leaders need AI they can audit, explain, and defend. Accuracy, explainability, and governance have to be architectural, built into the platform from the model layer right through to model behavior," said Rob Clark, President of Seekr. "Being recognized among the most innovative AI companies in the world reflects what our customers value most and where the market is heading."

## Government and Defense Traction

Seekr"s customer base reads like a roster of high-stakes government buyers. SeekrFlow is already deployed at the U.S. Army, U.S. Navy, and U.S. Special Operations Command. The platform is also available to the Department of Defense and other federal agencies through AWS GovCloud via the AWS Marketplace. A recent collaboration with General Dynamics Information Technology aims to accelerate the development of secure, trusted agentic AI solutions across federal agencies.

The government focus is not incidental. The U.S. Office of Management and Budget"s M-25-21 guidance now requires federal AI systems to provide explainability and audit trails. Across the Atlantic, the EU AI Act -- expected to become enforceable in 2026 -- imposes similar obligations on high-risk AI deployed in Europe. Seekr"s partnership with London-based Arcas to deliver sovereign AI positions the company to capture European demand as compliance deadlines approach.

## A Leadership Expansion Signals Scaling Ambitions

The CB Insights recognition comes amid a broader organizational build-out. Seekr recently appointed Colby Proffitt, the 2024 Startup CMO of the Year, as Chief Marketing Officer to accelerate its go-to-market strategy across both enterprise and government channels. The company has also added a new Chief Revenue Officer, Chief People Officer, and VP for Army and SOCOM Programs -- a hiring spree that suggests Seekr is gearing up for a wave of product announcements and contract wins.

## Analysis: Why Explainability Is Becoming Table Stakes

The market for AI governance tools is expanding rapidly. IDC projects global spending on AI governance to exceed \$12 billion by 2028, growing at a 28 percent compound annual growth rate. Gartner estimates that 68 percent of enterprise buyers plan to prioritize AI solutions with built-in explainability within the next twelve months. These numbers reflect a fundamental shift: as AI moves from experimental pilots to production deployments in regulated industries, the ability to prove why a model made a specific decision is no longer a nice-to-have feature -- it is a procurement requirement.

Major cloud providers like AWS, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Azure have introduced responsible AI toolkits, but these tend to be add-ons to general-purpose model-training services. Seekr"s purpose-built architecture for auditability, combined with its existing footprint in defense and government, gives it an advantage in a compliance-driven market that larger vendors have been slower to address.

The CB Insights AI 100 nod validates that the market is paying attention. For enterprises weighing AI investments, the message is clear: in 2026, the most innovative AI is not just the most powerful -- it is the most accountable.
