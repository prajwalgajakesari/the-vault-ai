# Dust Raises $40 Million Series B to Build Multiplayer AI Agents for Enterprise

Most companies have spent the last three years bolting AI assistants onto individual workflows. One employee prompts a chatbot, gets an answer, and the context vanishes into a private thread. The result is real but isolated productivity — gains that never compound across teams.

Paris-based startup Dust thinks the fix is architectural, not incremental. On May 18, the company announced a $40 million Series B led by Abstract and Sequoia, with strategic participation from Snowflake Ventures and Datadog Ventures. The round brings Dust's total funding to more than $60 million and will bankroll an ambitious push into what co-founder and CEO Gabriel Hubert calls "multiplayer AI."

## From Single-Player to Multiplayer

The phrase is deliberately borrowed from gaming. In single-player mode, every employee operates their own AI assistant in isolation: the sales rep researches a prospect, the solutions engineer runs discovery the next day from scratch, and the product marketer drafts a one-pager that the content writer has never seen. Knowledge stays trapped in individual sessions.

Dust's platform flips that model. It provides shared workspaces where humans and AI agents operate side by side with access to the same context, tools, notifications, and goals. Agents are not private utilities — they are organizational resources that any authorized team member can build, share, invoke, and improve. The company positions itself as an "operating system for AI agents" rather than another chatbot wrapper.

"What will transform the way we work isn't the next best model or assistant," Hubert said in the company's announcement. He argued that the breakthrough will come from systems that give humans and agents shared, governed access to the same information so they become genuine collaborators.

## Traction That Caught Sequoia's Attention

The numbers backing the pitch are hard to dismiss. Dust says more than 3,000 organizations now use the platform, with over 300,000 agents deployed across customers. Weekly active usage sits at 70 percent, and the company reported zero churn throughout 2025 — a retention figure that would be exceptional in any enterprise SaaS category, let alone one as nascent as agentic AI.

Konstantine Buhler, a partner at Sequoia, pointed to those engagement metrics as proof that the product has moved beyond experimentation. Ramtin Naimi, general partner at Abstract, highlighted how customers such as Datadog and 1Password are not just using Dust but actively building agents that collaborate across departments and learn from every interaction.

Named customers span a range of use cases. At Clay, Dust serves as foundational knowledge infrastructure for a fast-growing go-to-market team. At Persona, teams across 11 departments have deployed more than 300 agents. At Doctolib, the platform supports a company-wide AI strategy reaching 3,000 employees.

## The Founders' Playbook

Hubert and co-founder Stanislas Polu have been building together since meeting at Stanford in 2007. They previously co-founded TOTEMS, a data analytics company acquired by Stripe in 2014, and then spent five years at Stripe scaling products and teams. Polu later joined OpenAI as a research engineer on Greg Brockman's team, where he co-authored papers on AI reasoning with Ilya Sutskever. Hubert became Chief Product Officer at French healthtech company Alan.

In September 2022, Polu left OpenAI with a conviction that became Dust's founding thesis: the models were already powerful enough to be economically transformative, but were under-deployed because the product layer was missing. The pair launched Dust to build that layer.

## Under the Hood

Dust's platform connects to more than 100 enterprise data sources and integrates with tools teams already rely on — Slack, Snowflake, HubSpot, Notion, Gmail, and Google Drive among them. A hybrid intelligence layer combines semantic search for deep contextual understanding with MCP (Model Context Protocol) connections for real-time queries and actions across tools.

Built-in memory and reinforcement loops allow agents to learn from interactions and improve over time, while enterprise governance features provide granular permissions, cost monitoring, full audit trails, and agent analytics. The company is SOC 2 Type II certified and GDPR compliant with both EU and US data residency options.

## What Comes Next

Dust plans to deploy the new capital across three frontiers: agents that learn and improve automatically through use, collaboration primitives that make humans and agents equal co-contributors with bidirectional access to shared projects and context, and infrastructure that makes governance and orchestration predictable at enterprise scale.

The bet is that as AI capabilities continue to advance, the companies that pull ahead will not be those with the best individual assistants but those with the best systems for coordinating work across humans and agents at organizational scale. Dust is building the plumbing for that future — and with $60 million in the bank and zero churn on the books, it has a credible shot at defining the category.
