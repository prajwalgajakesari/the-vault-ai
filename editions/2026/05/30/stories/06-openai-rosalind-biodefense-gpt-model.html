# OpenAI Launches Rosalind Biodefense Program With GPT-Rosalind Life Sciences Model

OpenAI is embedding its most advanced biology-focused AI directly into the machinery of national pandemic defense. On May 29, the company unveiled the Rosalind Biodefense Program, a first-of-its-kind initiative that pairs its frontier life sciences model, GPT-Rosalind, with vetted government agencies, national laboratories, and global health organizations working to prevent the next pandemic.

The move marks the first time OpenAI has offered a specialized model free of charge to government partners at this scale, and it arrives against a backdrop of active biological crisis: the World Health Organization declared the Bundibugyo Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Uganda a public health emergency on May 17, and the strain has no licensed vaccine.

A Model Named for the Mother of Molecular Biology

GPT-Rosalind, first introduced in April 2026, is OpenAI's frontier reasoning model purpose-built for the life sciences. Named after Rosalind Franklin -- the X-ray crystallographer whose rigorous experimental work helped reveal the double-helix structure of DNA -- the model specializes in reasoning about molecules, proteins, genes, and disease-relevant biology. According to OpenAI's internal benchmarks, it outperforms GPT-5, GPT-5.2, and GPT-5.4 across chemistry, biochemistry, and experiment design tasks.

The model assists researchers with evidence synthesis, hypothesis generation, experiment design, and data analysis -- capabilities that translate directly into the kind of rapid scientific response that biodefense demands.

Two Tracks: Developers and Government

The Rosalind Biodefense Program operates on two parallel tracks. The first is a developer track that sponsors access to GPT-Rosalind for trusted developers building defensive tools in areas like epidemiological modeling, early detection, screening systems, and non-pharmaceutical interventions. Teams can apply for sponsorship and launch support, and the track is open globally to academic, nonprofit, government-affiliated, and mission-driven organizations.

The second is a government track that extends direct GPT-Rosalind access to select U.S. federal agencies and allied international partners with approved public health and biodefense missions. OpenAI describes the dual approach as "defensive acceleration," writing that it believes "frontier AI should meaningfully advantage those defenders."

On background, OpenAI says it briefed the White House and several federal agencies on the approach and is actively onboarding public-health-focused federal agencies.

National Lab Partnerships and CEPI

The launch partners read like a who's-who of institutional biosecurity. Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory is pairing GPT-Rosalind with its supercomputing and simulation infrastructure to design and evaluate medical countermeasures. Shankar Sundaram, who directs Livermore's Bioresilience Incubator, said the collaboration is "examining how advanced AI tools can help scientists interpret complex data and existing knowledge, identify stronger candidates."

Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory plans to integrate the model into a protein-engineering platform that screens mutant enzymes for therapeutics, countermeasures, and biothreat characterization.

The Coalition for Epidemic Preparedness Innovations (CEPI), a global partnership launched in 2017 to accelerate vaccine development, is applying GPT-Rosalind to its 100 Days Mission -- an ambitious program to compress the vaccine development timeline against emerging threats, including the current Ebola outbreak.

Deepening the Government Footprint

The Rosalind Biodefense Program does not exist in a vacuum. It deepens a government footprint OpenAI has built steadily through 2026: a $200 million Defense Department pilot program, active deployments at Los Alamos, Lawrence Livermore, and Sandia national laboratories, and an AWS GovCloud distribution deal that places OpenAI products across Amazon's public-sector cloud infrastructure, including classified environments for Secret and Top Secret workloads.

The timing carries political significance. The program launched just over a week after President Trump postponed an executive order that would have created a government review process for the most powerful AI models before their release. With that standardized framework shelved, OpenAI is effectively setting its own terms for early government access in the biology domain.

Budget Crosscurrents

The federal budget landscape adds another layer of complexity. Trump's fiscal 2027 budget request asks Congress for $1.5 trillion in defense spending and stakes $1.2 billion at the Department of Energy on AI infrastructure, enough for seven new supercomputers at Argonne and Oak Ridge national laboratories. But biodefense itself appears mostly in the cuts: the same request reduces HHS funding by 12.5 percent and trims the Administration for Strategic Preparedness and Response by $356 million, eliminating the Hospital Preparedness Program while sparing medical-countermeasure work and the Strategic National Stockpile.

That tension -- massive AI investment paired with reduced public health funding -- makes the Rosalind Biodefense Program both a strategic opportunity and a potential lifeline for pandemic preparedness work that might otherwise lose federal support.

The Bigger Picture

OpenAI has published a Preparedness Framework under which it classified its July 2025 ChatGPT Agent as high-capability in biology and shipped it with safeguards designed to minimize risk. The Rosalind Biodefense Program extends that philosophy from risk mitigation to active defense, arguing that the same capabilities that make advanced AI dangerous in biology also make it indispensable for protecting against biological threats.

Whether a private company should be the one deciding who gets frontier biology AI -- and on what terms -- remains an open question. But with an active Ebola emergency, no licensed vaccine for the circulating strain, and federal biodefense budgets shrinking, the practical argument for getting GPT-Rosalind into the hands of defenders is hard to dismiss.

The application form for the Rosalind Biodefense Program is now live on OpenAI's website.
