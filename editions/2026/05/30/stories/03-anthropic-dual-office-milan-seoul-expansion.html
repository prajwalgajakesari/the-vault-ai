# Anthropic Opens Milan and Seoul Offices in Same Week, Accelerating Global AI Expansion

In the span of 48 hours this week, Anthropic planted flags on two continents, opening an office in Milan on May 27 and formally appointing a country head for its Seoul operation on May 26. The back-to-back moves bring the AI safety company's global footprint to 12 cities and signal an aggressive international expansion strategy that is reshaping the competitive map for frontier AI well beyond Silicon Valley.

Seoul: A Market That Punches Above Its Weight

The Korea announcement centered on the appointment of KiYoung Choi as Representative Director of Anthropic Korea. Choi brings more than three decades of technology leadership across the Asia-Pacific region, having served as General Manager of Snowflake Korea before joining Anthropic. His resume reads like a directory of enterprise tech's biggest names: country leadership roles at Google Cloud, Adobe, Autodesk, and Microsoft Korea.

The hire reflects just how seriously Anthropic takes the Korean market. According to the company's Economic Index, Korean users engage with Claude at more than 3.5 times the rate expected for the country's population size, with usage concentrated in technical and creative work. Korea ranks in the top five globally for both total and per capita Claude usage. The developer community is particularly robust -- a Korean software engineer currently holds the title of the world's top Claude Code user, and active weekly Claude Code users in Korea grew sixfold in just four months.

"Korea is one of the most sophisticated AI markets in the world, leading in hardware innovation, developer activity, and enterprise adoption," Choi said. "Korean organizations combine technical depth with a commitment to responsible deployment, which is exactly where Anthropic operates. That alignment is what brought me to Anthropic -- and why we are focused on building in Korea for the long term."

Major Korean enterprises are already building with Claude. SK Telecom, the country's largest telecommunications company, chose Claude to build a custom AI customer service model that has become a blueprint for the telecom industry. Law&Company uses Claude to power its AI legal assistant, helping lawyers cut time spent on legal research and document preparation while maintaining high accuracy.

Seoul becomes Anthropic's third office in the Asia-Pacific region, following Tokyo and Bengaluru. The region's run-rate revenue has grown over 10x in the past year, and large business accounts -- each representing more than $100,000 in annual run-rate revenue -- have increased eightfold.

Milan: Europe's Sixth Outpost, With Industrial Muscle

One day after the Korea announcement, Anthropic opened its Milan office -- the company's sixth in Europe, joining London, Dublin, Paris, Zurich, and Munich. The speed of the European buildout is striking: just six months ago, Anthropic had only London and Dublin on the continent. It now covers six countries.

The Milan launch came with an unusually detailed roster of enterprise customers. Generali Group and Unipol Group anchor the financial services sector. Angelini Pharma and Bracco Group represent life sciences. Enel Group provides the energy utility presence, and Pirelli adds automotive credibility. Italian tech companies have emerged as early power users: Satispay, the financial super-app with more than six million users, used Claude to compress an 18-month engineering roadmap into seven months. At Bending Spoons, one of Italy's largest technology companies, the majority of code changes are now co-authored with Claude Code.

Anthropic also partnered with JAKALA, a Milan-based data-and-AI consultancy, deploying Claude across more than 3,000 seats to free up roughly 70 percent of senior team time for higher-judgment client work.

Chris Ciauri, Managing Director of International at Anthropic, framed the move broadly: "We are here to support Italian enterprise, Italian research, and Italian culture through a safe AI transition. Italy is a country that has always embraced profound transformation and we are optimistic about what frontier AI can do for this country."

The Milan opening carries a distinct cultural dimension. It closely followed the release of Magnifica Humanitas, Pope Leo XIV's first encyclical and the first papal teaching dedicated to artificial intelligence. Anthropic co-founder Chris Olah was invited to speak at its Vatican presentation, positioning the company as the frontier AI lab most willing to publicly engage with institutional ethical frameworks.

Why This Matters

The dual-office launch is more than geographic box-checking. It reflects a deliberate strategy to localize AI deployment in markets where demand is already proven, rather than building offices and hoping customers follow.

In Korea, the data spoke first: 3.5x over-indexing on usage relative to population is the kind of signal that compels physical investment. In Italy, enterprise contracts with the likes of Generali, Enel, and Pirelli preceded the office, not the other way around. EMEA is now Anthropic's fastest-growing region, with run-rate revenue up roughly 9x and large-business accounts up 10x year-on-year.

The competitive implications are significant. OpenAI does not have a Milan office. Google's Italian footprint runs primarily through advertising and Cloud sales teams. In Korea, the appointment of a seasoned enterprise executive like Choi -- rather than a research or policy lead -- signals that Anthropic is prioritizing commercial traction alongside its safety positioning.

The expansion also arrives in the same week Anthropic announced its $65 billion Series H funding round at a $965 billion post-money valuation, providing the financial firepower to sustain a global office buildout at this pace.

What to Watch

Senior Anthropic leadership will travel to Seoul in coming weeks to formally open the office and meet with customers, which could yield additional enterprise partnership announcements. In Milan, the key question is operational scale -- Anthropic has not disclosed headcount or hiring targets for the new location. With 12 offices now spanning four continents and a near-trillion-dollar valuation to fund the push, the question is no longer whether Anthropic can compete globally with OpenAI and Google, but whether its safety-first positioning and enterprise-heavy strategy will prove to be a durable differentiator in markets where AI adoption is accelerating fastest.
