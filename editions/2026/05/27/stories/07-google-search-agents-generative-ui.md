# Google Search Enters Agent Era With Background Information Agents and Custom Generative UI

Google has unveiled the most sweeping overhaul of its flagship Search product in more than 25 years, replacing the iconic search box with an AI-powered interface and introducing always-on background agents that monitor the web around the clock. Announced at Google I/O 2026, the update transforms Search from a reactive query engine into a proactive, agent-driven platform powered by Gemini 3.5 Flash, and signals the company's most aggressive move yet to defend its dominance against a surging wave of AI-native competitors.

Elizabeth Reid, Google's vice president and head of Search, called it 'the biggest upgrade to our iconic search box since its debut over 25 years ago.' The redesigned input field dynamically expands to accept text, images, files, video, and even open Chrome tabs, abandoning the keyword-first paradigm that defined web search for a generation. Rather than returning the familiar grid of blue links, the system drops users into AI-generated interactive experiences complete with custom visualizations, tools, and what Google is calling 'information agents.'

## Agents That Never Sleep

The headline feature is information agents: autonomous AI systems that operate continuously in the background, reasoning across blogs, news sites, social media posts, and real-time data feeds covering finance, shopping, and sports. Users describe what they care about in natural language, and the agent monitors for relevant changes, delivering synthesized updates with the ability to take action. Google described the agents as capable of continuously scanning apartment listings against specific requirements, or alerting sneaker enthusiasts the instant a favorite athlete announces a collaboration drop.

The company is also expanding agentic booking capabilities across local experiences and services. Users can describe detailed criteria, such as finding a private karaoke room for six on a Friday night that serves food late, and Search will assemble pricing, availability, and direct booking links. For select categories like home repair, beauty, and pet care, Google will even call businesses on behalf of users. Information agents will launch this summer for Google AI Pro and Ultra subscribers in the United States, while the redesigned search box began rolling out globally on May 20 in all countries and languages where AI Mode is available.

## Custom Generative UI and Mini Apps

Equally ambitious is the integration of Google's Antigravity agentic coding platform directly into Search. Powered by Gemini 3.5 Flash, the system can generate custom user interfaces on the fly: interactive calculators, data visualizations, simulations, graphs, and trackers tailored to individual queries. Ask about mortgage rates and a live calculator appears; ask about a hiking trail and an interactive elevation map materializes. Google said these generative UI capabilities will be available to everyone this summer, free of charge.

For ongoing tasks, Search goes further. Users can ask it to build persistent mini apps, custom fitness dashboards that pull in weather data, maps, meal plans, and local reviews, or wedding planning trackers that consolidate venue availability, calendar events, and budget spreadsheets. These mini apps, built through Antigravity's coding capabilities, will roll out first for Google AI Pro and Ultra subscribers in the coming months.

## The Numbers Behind the Shift

The scale of the transition is staggering. AI Mode, the conversational search interface launched just one year ago, has surpassed one billion monthly users, with query volumes more than doubling every quarter since launch. AI Overviews now reach 2.5 billion monthly users. Google noted that last quarter, total search queries hit an all-time high, a data point the company uses to counter the narrative that AI summaries are cannibalizing traditional search traffic.

The company is also expanding Personal Intelligence in AI Mode to nearly 200 countries and territories across 98 languages, with no subscription required. Users can connect Gmail, Google Photos, and soon Google Calendar, giving Search personal context to deliver more relevant results.

## Why This Matters

Google's announcement represents a fundamental reimagining of what a search engine is. For publishers and the broader web ecosystem, the implications are significant. If Search can synthesize information, build interactive tools, and dispatch background agents to track changes, the incentive for users to click through to source websites shrinks further. Referral traffic from Google has already been declining as AI Overviews expanded, and these new features are likely to accelerate that trend.

The competitive pressure is intense. OpenAI's ChatGPT reported 900 million weekly active users in early 2026, while Perplexity AI and Microsoft Copilot continue to push conversational AI as alternatives to conventional web search. Google's response is not to retreat into its legacy format but to push deeper into agentic territory, betting that scale, at 2.5 billion monthly users, remains a moat few competitors can approach.

Regulatory scrutiny is also mounting. The European Commission published measures in April requiring Google to share anonymized search data with rival search engines and AI chatbot providers under the Digital Markets Act, with a compliance deadline of July 27, 2026. The more Google's search interface resembles a self-contained AI application, the sharper that scrutiny is likely to become.

## What to Watch Next

The summer rollout timeline will be critical. Information agents launching behind a paid subscription wall suggests Google is still calibrating the economics and reliability of always-on AI monitoring at scale. Whether these agents can deliver on the promise of 24/7 intelligent web monitoring without drowning users in noise or hallucinated updates will determine whether this is a genuine paradigm shift or an expensive feature that quietly retreats to the margins. Meanwhile, publishers, advertisers, and regulators will be watching closely to see how much further Google can push the boundaries of Search before the open web pushes back.
