# Anthropic Opens Milan Office as EMEA Revenue Surges Ninefold Year Over Year

Anthropic is planting its flag in Italy this month, opening a Milan office that adds a sixth European city to its international footprint — and the numbers behind the move are striking. The AI safety company's run-rate revenue across the Europe, Middle East, and Africa region has grown more than ninefold in the past year, making EMEA its fastest-growing market globally.

The expansion is the latest chapter in an aggressive international push that has transformed Anthropic from a U.S.-focused research lab into a company scrambling to staff up on two continents simultaneously. With offices already operating in London (approximately 200 staff), Dublin, Zurich, Paris, and Munich, Milan becomes the newest node in a network that is expanding faster than at any point in the company's four-year history.

## The Numbers Behind the Push

The revenue figures Anthropic is citing for EMEA are hard to ignore. Run-rate revenue in the region is up roughly 9x year over year. The number of large business accounts — enterprise customers generating meaningful contract values — has grown more than 10x over the same period. Together, those metrics suggest the company is not merely adding logos; it is landing deals at scale.

That trajectory tracks with Anthropic's overall growth story. The company's global run-rate revenue expanded from approximately $87 million at the start of 2024 to $5 billion by August 2025 — a roughly 57x increase in under two years. EMEA appears to be running even hotter than that already-extraordinary headline rate.

To keep pace, Anthropic has committed to tripling its international headcount and expanding its applied AI team fivefold before year-end. The company is hiring 100 new roles out of its London and Dublin offices alone.

## Italy as Strategic Beachhead

The choice of Milan is deliberate. Italy's economy is the third largest in the eurozone, and its industrial base — spanning financial services, manufacturing, luxury goods, and consumer products — represents exactly the enterprise buyer profile Anthropic is targeting. The existing Paris-based EMEA South team has been running early-cycle account management in Italy ahead of the local hires landing in Milan.

"After France and Germany, Italy is a natural next step," said Chris Ciauri, Anthropic's managing director of international, in an interview with Italy's Il Corriere della Sera. The quote, characteristically understated, belies the scale of what Anthropic is attempting: building a pan-European enterprise sales and support infrastructure at speed, while simultaneously navigating a complex regulatory environment.

Ciauri himself is a notable hire. A veteran of some of the most successful international enterprise expansions in recent tech history, he previously served as president of EMEA at Google Cloud and executive vice president and general manager of EMEA at Salesforce, where he helped scale that region from under $200 million to over $3 billion in revenue during a decade-long tenure. His appointment signals that Anthropic is not treating international expansion as an afterthought.

## The Broader European Context

The Milan opening arrives as European enterprises are under pressure to deploy AI tools to remain competitive, while governments across the continent debate the appropriate regulatory framework for the technology. Anthropic has been vocal on both fronts.

Co-founder Daniela Amodei has publicly urged governments to act early on AI regulation, drawing a direct comparison to social media platforms, where policymakers waited until harms were entrenched before intervening. The company has also clashed with the U.S. administration over the use of its models for military applications, insisting on guardrails that restrict autonomous weapons targeting and domestic surveillance use cases.

That ethical positioning may be a selling point in Europe, where enterprise procurement teams are increasingly attentive to AI governance, and where regulatory frameworks such as the EU AI Act create compliance obligations that favor vendors with credible safety track records.

## Analysis: What the Milan Move Signals

Taken together, the data points and the geography tell a consistent story. Anthropic is not dabbling in Europe — it is building for the long term.

The sequencing of office openings (London, Dublin, Zurich, then Paris and Munich, now Milan) mirrors the playbook of enterprise software companies that have successfully scaled across the continent: start with English-language markets, add financial hubs, then move into major industrial economies. That Anthropic is following a template well-worn by Salesforce — a company Ciauri helped scale across the same geography — is unlikely to be coincidental.

The 10x growth in large enterprise accounts is particularly telling. Enterprise AI adoption has historically lagged consumer deployment by 18 to 24 months, as procurement cycles, security reviews, and integration work slow the process. If Anthropic's EMEA enterprise account count is already growing at that rate, it suggests the company may be catching the earliest wave of large-scale enterprise commitments — deals that tend to be sticky and expand over time.

The financial services and industrial sectors Anthropic has identified as Italian targets are also sectors where the company has been building vertical-specific tooling. Anthropic has released agent templates for financial services workflows — including pitchbook construction, know-your-customer file screening, and month-end close processes — following earlier releases targeting healthcare and legal. A Milan office staffed with local enterprise experts would be positioned to sell directly into the banks and manufacturers headquartered in Italy's financial capital.

## What Comes Next

Anthropic's European expansion is not finished. The company's stated goal of tripling international headcount implies significant additional hiring across its existing European hubs, even before new markets are factored in. The company has also established its first Asia-Pacific presence in Tokyo, suggesting that the geographic rollout that brought it to Milan is continuing into new regions.

For European enterprises still evaluating AI vendors, the pace of Anthropic's buildout matters. Local offices mean local support, local compliance expertise, and local relationships — factors that carry weight in procurement decisions, particularly in regulated industries. The ninefold revenue growth suggests that message is landing.

Italy gets its Anthropic office this month. The rest of Europe is already paying attention.
