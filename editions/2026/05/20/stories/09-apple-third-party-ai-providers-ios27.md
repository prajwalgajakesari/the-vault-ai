# Apple Reportedly Opening Apple Intelligence to Google and Anthropic as Third-Party AI Providers

For the past eighteen months, OpenAI held an unusual distinction: it was the only outside AI company woven into the fabric of Apple's operating system. That arrangement is about to end. According to multiple reports published earlier this month, Apple is preparing to open Apple Intelligence to third-party AI providers — including Google and Anthropic — through a new system-level framework arriving with iOS 27 this fall.

The shift is the most consequential change to Apple's AI strategy since the company first announced its partnership with OpenAI at WWDC 2024. Where that deal gave ChatGPT a privileged lane into Siri and Writing Tools, the new architecture treats no single model as the default. Instead, users would be able to choose which AI powers their device — routing requests from Siri, Writing Tools, and Image Playground to whichever provider they prefer.

## The Extensions Framework

The mechanism Apple is building is called "Extensions." Rather than baking specific models into the OS at the firmware level, Extensions would allow AI providers to register their capabilities through App Store applications. Install the Gemini app, open Settings under "Apple Intelligence & Siri," and you could designate Google's model to handle your writing tasks. Do the same with the Claude app, and Anthropic's model takes the wheel. Apple's own foundation models would remain the default, but the choice would be genuinely open.

The scope is wide. Compatible third-party models would be able to take over not just conversational Siri queries but also deeper integrations: rewriting documents in Writing Tools, generating images in Image Playground, and powering the AI-driven Shortcuts that iOS 27 is expected to introduce. Apple is also reportedly planning distinct Siri voices for different AI models, so users can audibly tell whether they're talking to Apple's model, Google's Gemini, or Anthropic's Claude — a small but telling detail that signals Apple is thinking carefully about how to surface provider identity without creating confusion.

Apple declined to comment publicly. However, people familiar with the matter told 9to5Mac that the company is already conducting closed-door testing with both Google and Anthropic integrations, and that the feature is expected to be announced at the Worldwide Developers Conference on June 8, 2026.

## Why Now

The timing reflects a company under competitive pressure it has been slow to acknowledge publicly. Apple Intelligence launched with iOS 18 in late 2024 to a muted reception. Critics noted that Siri's underlying capabilities lagged far behind the conversational fluency of standalone models like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude. The ChatGPT partnership helped at the margins, but it did little to close the perception gap — and it came with an inherent ceiling: Apple could only be as good as the one partner it had chosen.

Opening the platform to multiple providers addresses that problem structurally. "Apple is turning the iPhone into a neutral host for whichever AI a user prefers," noted TechRadar's analysis of the reports. "That's a fundamentally different bet than trying to win the AI race outright."

The move also coincides with Google's own intensifying push on Android. Google I/O 2026, held earlier this month, showcased deep Gemini integrations across Android 16 that analysts described as the most cohesive AI-in-OS story either major platform had told. Apple's willingness to let Gemini run inside iOS — on a device sold by a direct competitor — suggests the company has concluded that ecosystem openness is more valuable than keeping Google's model off its platform.

## What It Means for Anthropic and Google

For Anthropic, an iOS 27 integration would represent a meaningful distribution win. Claude has built a strong reputation among professional users for nuanced writing assistance and careful reasoning, but it has largely reached consumers through the web and standalone apps rather than deep OS-level access. An Extensions partnership would put Claude inside the daily workflows of hundreds of millions of iPhone users — precisely the kind of ambient, habitual touchpoint that drives model preference and brand loyalty.

For Google, the calculus is more complex. The company is simultaneously trying to establish Gemini as the defining intelligence layer on Android while negotiating to appear as an option on a rival's platform. Google's willingness to participate reflects the commercial reality that iOS users represent a premium demographic it cannot afford to cede to OpenAI or Anthropic by default.

"The companies that can get their models into the flow of daily tasks — not just answering queries but rewriting emails, generating images, summarizing documents — are the ones that will shape how people think about AI," said one industry analyst tracking the negotiations. "Apple's platform is too large to ignore."

## OpenAI's Position

The reports do not suggest Apple intends to remove ChatGPT from iOS. OpenAI's model is expected to remain one of the available Extensions options. But the arrangement that gave ChatGPT first-mover status inside Apple Intelligence — announced at WWDC 2024 and shipped with iOS 18.2 in December of that year — will effectively end its privileged position. ChatGPT becomes one option among several rather than the sole outside voice in the ecosystem.

That is a significant symbolic change even if ChatGPT retains a large user base. It signals that Apple sees AI providers as a commodity layer it can orchestrate rather than a strategic partner it depends on — a dynamic that gives Apple considerably more leverage in future negotiations.

## The Bigger Picture

Apple's Extensions framework, if it ships as described, would represent one of the more important platform decisions in the company's recent history. It acknowledges, implicitly, that no single AI model — including Apple's own — is good enough at everything to justify a closed system. And it bets that users given a genuine choice will stay within the Apple ecosystem even as they pick the intelligence layer that suits them best.

WWDC begins June 8. Apple is expected to present the full Extensions architecture to developers then, with a public release accompanying iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 in fall 2026. The developer preview will be the first real test of how cleanly the integration works — and whether Google and Anthropic have built experiences compelling enough to pull users away from the defaults Apple sets.

The AI platform war is no longer just about which model is the most capable. It is increasingly about which models can get close enough to users to matter. Apple just opened a new front.
