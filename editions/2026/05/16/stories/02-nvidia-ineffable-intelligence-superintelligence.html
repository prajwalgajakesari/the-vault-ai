# Nvidia Partners With Ineffable Intelligence to Build Next Frontier of AI Superintelligence

A London-based startup with a record-breaking seed round and a DeepMind legend at its helm just locked in an engineering alliance with the world's most valuable chip company — and the implications for the next phase of AI may be profound.

On May 13, 2026, Nvidia announced an engineering-level partnership with Ineffable Intelligence, a British AI startup founded by David Silver — the UCL professor and former leader of Google DeepMind's reinforcement learning team, best known for creating AlphaGo. The two companies plan to co-design a large-scale infrastructure for reinforcement learning (RL) systems: AI that learns through trial and error rather than from vast troves of human-generated text and data.

The deal arrives just weeks after Ineffable Intelligence emerged from stealth with a headline-grabbing $1.1 billion seed round — the largest seed round in European history — at a valuation of $5.1 billion. That round was co-led by Sequoia Capital and Lightspeed Venture Partners, with strategic backing from Nvidia itself, Google, DST Global, Index Ventures, and the U.K.'s Sovereign AI Fund.

What Nvidia and Ineffable Are Building

The partnership is explicitly engineering-level, meaning teams from both companies are working side by side — not simply exchanging capital or signing a commercial agreement. Engineers from Nvidia and Ineffable Intelligence are jointly building a pipeline designed to feed reinforcement learning systems at unprecedented scale.

The technical work will begin on Nvidia's Grace Blackwell platform — the current generation of chips powering frontier AI workloads — and is expected to be among the first to explore Nvidia's next-generation Vera Rubin architecture. That timeline suggests Ineffable is being positioned not just as a customer, but as a co-design partner helping Nvidia understand what the hardware requirements of next-generation RL infrastructure actually look like.

Jensen Huang, Nvidia's founder and CEO, framed the collaboration in terms that signal a broader strategic bet. "The next frontier of AI is superlearners — systems that learn continuously from experience," Huang said. The word "superlearners" has since become shorthand inside the industry for AI agents that generate new knowledge through environmental feedback, rather than compressing existing human knowledge into model weights.

Silver's Vision: Beyond Human Data

David Silver has spent more than a decade arguing that reinforcement learning — not supervised learning from human data — is the path to genuine machine intelligence. His work on AlphaGo and AlphaZero demonstrated that RL systems, given the right environment and enough compute, could exceed human performance in domains where no sufficient human dataset even existed.

Ineffable Intelligence is building on that insight at a new scale and with a new ambition: creating a "superlearner" capable of discovering knowledge across open-ended domains, not just games with well-defined rules.

"Researchers have largely solved the easier problem of AI: how to build systems that know all the things humans already know," Silver said at the time of the seed round announcement. "But now we need to solve the harder problem of AI: how to build systems that discover new knowledge for themselves. That requires a very different approach — systems that learn from experience."

That framing is a direct challenge to the prevailing paradigm in commercial AI, where scaling up training data and model parameters has driven most of the industry's recent gains. Silver is betting that the next leap requires a fundamentally different engine.

The Market Context

Ineffable is not operating in a vacuum. A wave of well-funded startups — many founded by former researchers from DeepMind, OpenAI, and Meta AI — are pursuing alternatives to the large language model scaling playbook that has dominated the field since 2020.

Advanced Machine Intelligence, founded by Meta AI's former chief scientist Yann LeCun, raised $1.03 billion for AI systems built around world models and reasoning. Recursive Superintelligence, another London-based lab drawing on DeepMind and OpenAI alumni, has attracted significant funding for self-improving AI research. Ineffable now joins that cohort with the deepest pockets of the group at seed stage and a direct hardware partnership with the dominant supplier of AI compute.

Nvidia's role is also worth examining. The company has previously backed OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral, and Cohere, among others — but those were financial investments. The Ineffable deal is something different: a technical collaboration in which Nvidia engineers are actively building alongside the startup. That level of engagement suggests Nvidia sees reinforcement learning infrastructure as a strategic frontier for its own platform, not merely a promising portfolio bet.

Analysis: A Hardware-Software Flywheel for RL

The partnership creates a potential flywheel that goes beyond the immediate collaboration. If Ineffable Intelligence succeeds in scaling reinforcement learning to produce genuinely novel AI capabilities, the compute demands will be enormous — and almost entirely dependent on Nvidia hardware. Conversely, by embedding its engineers in Ineffable's infrastructure work, Nvidia ensures its future chip architectures are shaped by the requirements of RL workloads, not just the transformer-based training runs that currently dominate its data center revenue.

This dynamic is consistent with Nvidia's broader strategy under Jensen Huang: cement platform dominance not just by selling chips, but by becoming indispensable to the research agenda of the labs most likely to define what comes next. With Grace Blackwell already in production and Vera Rubin on the horizon, Nvidia is positioning itself at the center of whatever the post-LLM era demands.

There is also a geopolitical dimension. Ineffable is headquartered in London, and the U.K.'s Sovereign AI Fund is a backer — a signal that the British government views the startup as a national AI champion. The partnership with a U.S. company anchors that project to the dominant American AI hardware ecosystem, raising questions about how the relationship will evolve if regulatory or trade tensions complicate cross-Atlantic technology collaboration.

What Comes Next

Ineffable Intelligence has a relatively narrow window to demonstrate that its approach is more than theoretical. The company has capital, credibility, a founding team with deep research pedigree, and now a hardware partner with the resources to help it scale. What it does not yet have is a product, a revenue base, or a publicly demonstrated system.

The AI industry's attention will be focused on whether Silver and his team can translate the elegance of AlphaGo's reinforcement learning approach into something that works at the messy scale of real-world domains — and whether Nvidia's bet on "superlearners" proves as prescient as its earlier bets on deep learning and generative AI.

If it works, the implications extend well beyond Ineffable's valuation. A genuinely scalable RL infrastructure could accelerate scientific discovery, autonomous systems, and decision-making in ways that current LLM-based approaches cannot reach. That is precisely the kind of outcome that justifies both a $1.1 billion seed round and a partnership with the world's most powerful chip company.
